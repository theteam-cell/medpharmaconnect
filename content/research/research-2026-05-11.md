# Research notes — 2026-05-11

## 1. Physician mortgage rates — week of May 11, 2026

- Freddie Mac PMMS 30-yr fixed averaged **6.37%** week of May 7, 2026 — the second straight weekly uptick from 6.30% the week prior, but still roughly 39 bp below spring 2025.
- Physician-loan band remains **mid-6% to low-7%** (effective working range 6.25%–7.00%) with a 0.125%–0.50% "doctor tax" premium over comparable conventional pricing on identical files.
- Lender dispersion on identical files is still **0.25%–0.50%**, meaning the cheap quote and the expensive quote on the same borrower can be 30+ bp apart. Industry guidance continues to point to a 3–5 lender shop saving $50K+ over a 30-year term.
- Rate consensus for the next 90 days (May–July 2026) is **5.9%–6.5%**, holding low-to-mid 6% with episodic 20–50 bp wiggles driven by oil/CPI volatility. Spring CPI print at **3.3% YoY** plus Middle East tensions are the main upward pressures.
- Physician Bank, Wealthkeel, SalaryDr, and LeverageRx 2026 guides all flag the same structural truth: physician loans are **portfolio products** held on bank balance sheets, so pricing reflects each bank's deposit/relationship targets — not the agency MBS curve. That's why two banks can quote the same file 0.50% apart on the same day.

## 2. Dentist-specific eligibility and student-loan landscape

- Average **dental-school debt for the class of 2025 was ~$297,800**, with dental school alone accounting for ~$280,300 (educationdata.org). The class of 2026 is tracking similarly.
- About **82%–84% of dental graduates carry educational debt at graduation**, and the practical median for indebted dentists has been sitting in the **$280K–$310K** range for three consecutive cycles.
- This matters for physician mortgages because **most dental professionals qualify** for the same product (programs typically list "DDS / DMD" alongside MD/DO), but the **product economics work differently for dentists** than for hospital-employed MDs:
  - Many dentists are 1099 / practice-owners within 2–3 years of graduation, which triggers the 2-year self-employment underwriting rule that physician-loan programs only partially waive.
  - PSLF eligibility is rare for private-practice dentists, so the new July 1, 2026 RAP (Repayment Assistance Plan) treatment becomes the dominant student-loan-DTI variable.
- **July 1, 2026 federal student-loan overhaul (now ~7 weeks out)**: Grad PLUS eliminated for new borrowers; **$50K/year / $200K lifetime** professional-student cap; new IDR plans collapsing into **RAP** with **1%–10% of AGI** payments and **no cap**, up to 30 years (ADA / ASDA / ADEA confirming for dental cohort).
- ADA and ASDA are both running borrower-education campaigns urging current dental students and recent grads to lock in IDR documentation before the cutover; ADEA's loan-forgiveness tracker shows state-level NHSC-style dental loan repayment options still active in most states.
- Lender-side effect: **physician-mortgage banks are increasingly pre-underwriting projected RAP payments** on new-borrower files, not the current IDR payment — meaning a dentist with $290K of federal debt and a $220K starting salary could see DTI calculated against a $1,800–$2,200/month RAP estimate even if their actual payment today is far lower.

## 3. Housing-market overlay for high-income healthcare buyers, May 2026

- **New-home sales boost** in the latest release with median new-home price at the lowest since July 2021; resale inventory still **+8% YoY**, months-of-supply around **4.1**, the loosest since 2019 in many metros.
- Spring 2026 remains structurally **buyer-leaning** — 43% of agents busier than 2025, 80% of buyers no longer waiting for rate drops, ~1 in 3 sellers giving up sub-5% notes to list — but rates re-firming this week is putting modest pressure on payment math.
- Regional split persists: Midwest and Northeast running **seller-friendly**, South and West **buyer-friendly** with price cuts on ~50% of listings in Sarasota / Tampa / Phoenix.
- Consensus 2026 national price growth still **0%–3%** vs. **~3.6% wage growth** — the first year in five where wage growth outpaces home prices, which is the macro thesis behind the current buyer's window.

## Key takeaways for today's content

- The dentist audience is **underserved by physician-loan content** — most "physician loan" articles default to MD/DO scenarios and skip the dentist's three big complications: 1099 transition, lack of PSLF, and practice-loan + mortgage stack.
- The July 1 RAP clock now sits ~7 weeks out and is hitting dental borrowers harder than MD residents because dental income ramps faster but PSLF rarely applies.
- The rate backdrop (6.37% baseline, 0.25%–0.50% lender dispersion, 0.125%–0.50% doctor-tax premium) means dentists should still be shopping 3–5 lenders aggressively even if the spring "buyer's window" narrative is doing the buying-decision work for them.
