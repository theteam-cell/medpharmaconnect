# Research Notes — 2026-05-01

## 1. Mortgage rate environment ticked back up but spring window still open

- Freddie Mac PMMS put the 30-yr fixed at **6.30%** for the week ending Apr 30, 2026, up from **6.23%** the prior week (the lowest level of any spring homebuying season in three consecutive years). One year ago, the same survey read 6.76% — so even with this week's uptick, buyers are entering May ~46 bp lower than spring 2025.
- Physician-loan pricing remains a **band**, not a number: mid-6% to low-7% on 30-yr fixed depending on FICO bracket, LTV, and loan size. Physician-loan premium over comparable conventional is currently **0.125% – 0.50%**, and the spread between *the cheapest and most expensive physician-loan quotes on the same file* is also ~0.25% – 0.50%. That means the "physician-vs-conventional" decision and the "lender-vs-lender" decision can each move a doctor's payment more than a Fed meeting will.
- Volatility driver this week: oil prices jumped, pushing the 10-yr Treasury — and mortgage rates — higher. Rates remain rangebound 6.2%–6.5% on the 30-yr conventional, with the second-half 2026 outlook leaning modestly lower if inflation cools.

## 2. Lender competition is widening — Newrez's wholesale rollout is the headline

- **Newrez** opened its medical-professional loan to brokers via wholesale this spring — a meaningful shift, because it pushes a national non-bank physician program into the broker channel for the first time at scale. Brokers can now shop the Newrez doctor product alongside bank portfolio programs (BMO, Fifth Third, TD, First Horizon, Truist, etc.).
- For the borrower this means: a doctor working with a single bank loan officer is now competing against a broker who can quote 3–5 physician programs at once. Translation: **borrowers who don't shop are leaving real money on the table** — industry estimates put the lifetime cost of a single 0.25% – 0.50% rate gap at $25K–$50K+ over a 30-yr term on a $600K loan.
- Underwriting differences also stratifying: some lenders allow IBR/RAP payment for DTI, some require 1% of balance, some require fully amortizing. Same borrower, same offer letter — three very different DTIs.

## 3. Student-loan policy clock: ~9 weeks to July 1, 2026 cutover

- The **July 1, 2026 RAP transition** is now ~9 weeks away. Key impacts for physician borrowers:
  - **PSLF residency credit ends for new borrowers** post-July 1. Loans borrowed after that date won't have residency/fellowship years count toward the 120-payment threshold.
  - **Grad PLUS phaseout**: Federal Direct Unsubsidized capped at $50K/yr / $200K lifetime for professional students; Grad PLUS no longer available.
  - **RAP becomes the only IDR for new borrowers**, with payments ranging 1%–10% of AGI over up to 30 years. Critically, RAP will be the only PSLF-eligible plan for new borrowers.
- For mortgage qualification, the practical risk is for any physician who **takes out new loans (consolidation, refi into RAP, or new disbursements) post-July 1** — their DTI calc could change overnight. A SAVE/IBR holder with a $400/mo capped payment may see a lender-quoted DTI payment of $1,800+ if they're forced into RAP or a fully-amortizing alt.

## Implications for today's article

- Lender competition + rate stratification + a 9-week policy clock all point to the same advice: **shop the physician mortgage like you'd shop a clinical job offer — multiple quotes, side-by-side comparison, and a documented timeline.**
- Spring 2026 is a buyer-friendlier season than 2022–2024 (more inventory, slightly lower rates, real lender competition), which makes it a useful moment to teach the *shopping process* itself rather than just rate predictions.
