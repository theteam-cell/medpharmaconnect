# Research Notes — May 29, 2026

## 1. Physician Mortgage Market Expansion

Non-bank lenders are actively entering the physician mortgage niche. Redwood Trust issued its first residential mortgage-backed securitization for medical professionals on March 26, 2026 — a $482 million deal comprising 607 loans. Newrez has also launched a Medical Professional Home Loan available through retail, direct-to-consumer, wholesale, and joint venture channels.

Current physician loan rates sit in the **6.25%–7.0% range** (30-year fixed), roughly 0.125%–0.50% above conventional mortgages. Higher loan limits ($1M–$2M+) and no-PMI features remain the core draw.

Sources: National Mortgage News, SalaryDr, WealthKeel, PhysicianLiving

## 2. Major Student Loan Policy Changes Effective July 1, 2026

Sweeping federal student loan changes take effect July 1, 2026 and will significantly affect future physicians:

- **Borrowing caps**: Federal Direct Unsubsidized Loans capped at $50,000/year and $200,000 total. Grad PLUS eliminated.
- **PSLF changes**: Residents/fellows no longer receive PSLF credit during training years, effectively pushing forgiveness timelines 3–7 years further out.
- **New repayment plans**: Streamlined to two options — Tiered Standard Plan and Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP, the only income-driven option, up to 30-year term).
- **Existing borrowers largely grandfathered** under current rules.

Context: AAMC warns these changes could worsen the physician shortage. Vermont is actively offering state-level forgiveness to keep primary care doctors in-state.

Sources: AAMC, Medical Economics, WCAX (Vermont)

## 3. 2026 Housing Market — What High Earners Need to Know

- Redfin forecasts a "Great Housing Reset" — income growth outpacing home-price growth for the first time since the Great Recession.
- Median existing home price: **$396,800** (January 2026, NAR).
- Research from Fortune: income inequality at the top of the distribution is a primary driver of high home prices; high earners are simultaneously driving prices up and best-positioned to buy.
- "K-shaped housing market" dynamic: high-income buyers are expected to re-enter the market first as conditions stabilize.
- At current prices/rates, the rent-vs-buy math doesn't always favor buying even for high earners — but doctors with long time horizons and stable income are among the best candidates for homeownership.

Sources: Fortune, NewHomeSource, RealWealth, Ramsey Solutions
