# Research Notes — May 9, 2026

## 1. Physician mortgage rates and conventional benchmark this week
- Freddie Mac PMMS: 30-year fixed averaged **6.37%** as of May 7, 2026 (up from 6.30% the prior week, but still ~39 bp below the 6.76% reading from May 2025).
- Average 30-year fixed purchase mortgage on May 8, 2026 was **6.446%** (U.S. News tracker).
- Physician mortgage band still mid-6% to low-7%, with the **0.125%–0.50% "doctor tax"** premium over conventional intact and **0.25%–0.50% lender dispersion** on identical files.
- ARMs continue to price **0.50%–1.00%** below 30-year fixed physician products — the widest gap of the cycle.
- FICO bracketing matters more this spring: spread between a 680 and 760+ score is running **0.25%–0.50%** in pricing.
- Sources: Freddie Mac PMMS, U.S. News, DrLoanTool, Physician Living, WealthKeel, SalaryDr.

## 2. Cash reserves are the under-discussed underwriting lever
- Conventional rule of thumb: lenders typically expect **2–6 months of PITI(A)** in liquid post-closing reserves.
- Physician programs are markedly more flexible — many lenders advertise **0–6 months** required, and several waive reserves entirely for healthcare borrowers up to ~$2M in loan amount.
- Reserves get re-imposed when other underwriting risk shows up:
  - **Lower FICO** (e.g., low-700s/high-600s) — lenders often want **6–12 months** of reserves to offset.
  - **Future-income / pre-employment contract close** — lenders want enough reserves to cover the gap between first payment and first paycheck (commonly 3–6 months of PITI plus a buffer).
  - **Jumbo balances** (>$1M, sometimes >$1.5M) — even physician portfolio products tighten reserve asks at the top end.
  - **Self-employed / 1099 / partnership K-1** — extra reserves often replace 2-year tax-return seasoning.
- Eligible reserves: checking, savings, brokerage (typically discounted to ~70%), vested retirement (typically discounted to ~60%, and only counted if accessible). Not eligible: gift funds in transit, business accounts unless documented sole owner, crypto in many lenders' guidelines.
- Sources: Lower.com, Atlantic Bay, Doctor Loan Programs, MyPerfectMortgage, MD Preferred Network, Regions Bank physician guide.

## 3. Spring 2026 housing market is buyer-leaning but cautious
- 43% of agents report a busier spring than 2025; **80%** of buyers say they are no longer waiting for a rate drop before purchasing.
- "Comeback buyers" (re-entering after pausing in 2023–2024) are **~20%** of current shoppers.
- Sellers are finally letting go of historically low rates: **~1 in 3** sellers this spring is giving up a sub-5% mortgage to move; 35% of Coldwell-affiliated listings have sub-5% notes.
- Inventory continues to climb (months-of-supply ~4.1 — highest since 2019 in many metros).
- Median new-home prices are at the lowest level since July 2021, and new-home sales have ticked up.
- Net effect: buyers with strong financing (physicians, dual-income households, well-capitalized professionals) have more leverage than they did 12–18 months ago, but sellers still hold the line in Midwest/Northeast metros.
- Sources: HBS Dealer, Coldwell Banker spring report, NAR, Redfin 2026 outlook, U.S. News rate forecast.

## Implications for MedPharmaConnect audience
- The reserves conversation is overdue. Most physician-loan content focuses on the 0%-down headline; reserves are where deals actually fall apart for residents and new attendings closing on contract income.
- Pre-July 1 urgency continues, but the pressure is now on **documentation completeness** (reserves seasoning, IDR letters, PSLF certifications) more than on rates.
- Spring market dynamics favor disciplined physician buyers — the 5-lender shop, the dual-DTI stress test, and adequate reserves are more valuable than chasing the perfect rate.
