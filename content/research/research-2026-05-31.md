## 1. Rate environment (current)

- Sources: Freddie Mac PMMS; Bloomberg; AP via 10tv/Yahoo Finance; Physician Living; DrLoanTool.

## 2. Relocating-physician home buying (topic-of-day signal)
- Physician loan programs let a doctor **qualify on a signed employment contract/offer letter before the first paycheck**, and many allow closing **60–150 days before the start date** (varies by lender). Offer letter must state a start date (commonly within 90 days of closing), spell out base pay, position and structure, and carry **no contingencies** (no pending drug test, license, board exam, etc.).
- Contrast with FHA: HUD requires you to have started the job and received at least one pay stub before closing — physician programs are far more flexible for a cross-country move.
- Buying **sight unseen** is increasingly common and viable for relocating physicians who work with a physician-savvy local agent; relies on virtual tours, local inspection, and an experienced buyer's agent.
- 2026 relocation packages commonly include a **$20K–$50K signing bonus**, **$10K–$15K** moving reimbursement, and **1–3 months temporary housing** — which buys time to rent-first vs. buy-now.
- Affordability now a bigger relocation factor: home prices trending up in many metros mean a standard package may not stretch as far; cost-of-living-adjusted income matters more than headline salary.
- Sources: Physician Loans USA; The Mortgage Reports (offer-letter mortgage); AMA (physician employment offer); UrbanBound; MedSalaryData; PracticeLink; Jackson Physician Search.
