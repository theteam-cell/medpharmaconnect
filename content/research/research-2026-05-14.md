# Research Notes — 2026-05-14

## 1. Rate environment: 30-yr fixed firming, physician band sticky

- **Freddie Mac PMMS (week of May 7, 2026):** 30-year fixed averaged **6.37%**, second straight weekly uptick from 6.30% but still ~39 bp below spring 2025. PMMS results for the week ending May 14 release later on Thursday at 12 p.m. ET, so today's data is the May 7 print.
- **Daily indices, May 13, 2026 (Zillow):** 30-year purchase average **~6.51%**, drifting above the PMMS weekly figure as oil/CPI volatility continues.
- **Physician-loan band:** still mid-6% to low-7% with a **+0.125%–0.50%** "doctor tax" over conventional and **0.25%–0.50% lender dispersion** on identical files. Bankrate/WCI/Wealthkeel all reiterate the consensus that physician fixed rates stay in the 6.25%–7.0% band through 2026 with modest improvement only possible in H2.
- **FICO and loan-size pricing reminders for doctor borrowers:** 680→760+ spread is **0.25%–0.50%**, $1M+ files carry an additional **0.125%–0.25%** premium, condos +**0.125%**. Single-family detached still the best-priced collateral.
- **Implication:** the "wait for rates to drop" thesis hasn't paid out — 30-yr is up two weeks running. Buyers who locked late April at 6.30% beat today's quotes. Anyone running rent-vs-buy math should use 6.5%–6.75% as the working physician-loan rate, not the headline conventional figure.

## 2. Spring 2026 housing market: prices firm, inventory rising, buyer behavior shifting

- **Median sale price:** $417,700 in April (NAR data via TheStreet), an all-time April high but with growth moderating; **median list price** down **2.2% YoY** as sellers adjust.
- **Inventory:** **1.47 million unsold homes** at end of April, **+5.8% MoM and +1.4% YoY**. JPMorgan Global Research expects U.S. home prices to **stall at ~0%** in 2026. Some metros (Seattle: +31% total listings, +19.2% new listings MoM) running well above the national pace.
- **Cash-buyer share:** down 0.3% MoM as even affluent buyers move back to financing — relevant to physician-mortgage demand staying high through summer.
- **Strategic buyer shift:** Rocket Mortgage and Globe-and-Mail both reporting a structural shift away from "starter home as stepping stone." **Only 16% of 2025 buyers say they're buying a starter home as a tradable investment, vs. 29% in 2022**. One-third of first-time buyers now expect their first home to be their **forever home**. Implication for our audience: the traditional "buy starter, trade up in 5 years" playbook many residents/fellows are taught is statistically out of step with what buyers their age actually do in 2026.
- **High-income buyer note:** Median physician comp across specialties is ~**$427K** (25th pct $330K / 90th pct $800K) per StudentLoanPlanner — so physician borrowers have meaningfully more "upsize-vs-stretch" optionality than the median U.S. household.

## 3. RAP / July 1 student-loan overhaul: ~7 weeks out and shaping mortgage files now

- **RAP goes live July 1, 2026.** New federal borrowers using IDR can only use **RAP or the standard plan**; only RAP qualifies for **PSLF**. (NerdWallet, StudentLoanPlanner, Medical Economics, AAMC.)
- **Payment math:** RAP uses **AGI, not discretionary income**, tiered 1%–10% of AGI with a **$10/mo floor** and **principal guaranteed to drop $50/mo** thanks to a federal interest subsidy. No payment cap on the high end — that's the lever that swings physician DTI.
- **Loan caps for new borrowers:** Direct Unsubsidized **$50K/year / $200K lifetime** for graduate/professional students; **Grad PLUS eliminated**. Existing borrowers grandfathered but new-borrower mix changes for any incoming MS1/D1/PharmD class.
- **PSLF tightening:** new employer-disqualification authority and tighter administrative review remain on the table per StudentLoanPlanner/CollegeInvestor commentary; physicians counting on PSLF should re-certify employer eligibility before closing.
- **Mortgage implication:** Physician-mortgage banks are pre-underwriting projected RAP payments on files that will close after July 1, especially construction and long-window purchase files. On a high-AGI two-physician household with $400K+ of student debt, the swing in qualifying purchase price can be **$50K–$150K**. Get DTI math in writing under both current IDR and projected RAP.

## Today's article angle

The Rocket Mortgage / Globe-and-Mail "starter-home death" signal lines up well with a long-overdue topic: **starter home vs. forever home for new attendings in 2026.** It's a strategic article — not a product article — and it ties together rates (refi math harder than ever), market conditions (prices firm, supply slowly loosening), and student-loan policy (RAP DTI pressure on the upsize path) into a decision framework physicians actually use.
