### Item 1: Physician Mortgage Rates Holding in Mid-6% Range for 2026

Sources: [PhysicianLoansUSA](https://www.physicianloansusa.com/news/2026-housing-market-outlook), [PhysicianLiving](https://www.physicianliving.com/articles/post/mortgage-interest-rates), [DrLoanTool](https://www.drloantool.com/physician-mortgage-rates)

Physician mortgage fixed rates currently run approximately 0.125%–0.50% above comparable conventional rates. A 30-year fixed physician mortgage in early 2026 typically falls in the mid-6% to low-7% range, depending on LTV, FICO score, and loan amount. Rates are projected to remain in the 6.25%–7.0% range throughout 2026, with only modest declines possible by year-end. The rate premium is real, but for physicians carrying significant student debt, the tradeoffs — no PMI, flexible DTI treatment — remain favorable. An increasing number of banks and non-bank lenders are entering the physician loan space, making it a competitive environment for borrowers.

**Relevance:** Good context for any article on physician loan timing or rate comparisons.

### Item 2: PSLF Changes Take Effect July 1, 2026 — Big Implications for Doctors

Source: [Curbside Real Estate — PSLF is Changing in 2026](https://www.curbsiderealestate.com/pslf-is-changing-in-2026/)

Starting July 1, 2026, the Department of Education gains new authority to disqualify certain employers from PSLF (Public Service Loan Forgiveness) eligibility. For doctors at nonprofit hospitals, academic medical centers, and government clinics, this changes the calculus significantly. What was once a relatively stable 10-year forgiveness path now has new variables tied to employer status and policy shifts. Changes to repayment plans could also increase monthly payments under IDR plans — which directly affects debt-to-income ratios and, by extension, mortgage qualification.

**Relevance:** Very timely for the MedPharmaConnect audience. Many physicians pursuing PSLF are delaying homeownership — this news may accelerate or complicate that decision.

### Item 3: The "Great Housing Reset" — Income Growth Expected to Outpace Home Prices in 2026

Sources: [Fortune/Redfin](https://fortune.com/2025/12/06/housing-reset-income-growth-home-price-growth-2026-redfin/), [J.P. Morgan Housing Outlook](https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/global-research/real-estate/us-housing-market-outlook), [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/personal-finance/mortgages/article/housing-market-predictions-2026-165905235.html)

Redfin forecasts income growth outpacing home-price growth for the first time since the Great Recession era. J.P. Morgan projects national home prices to stall at approximately 0% growth in 2026. Mortgage rates are expected to drift into the low-6% range as inflation cools. Inventory has risen in most markets, though a structural housing deficit persists. For high earners like physicians, the affordability window may be meaningfully better in 2026 than it has been in several years — especially with physician loan programs that allow 0% down and exclude PMI.

**Relevance:** Strong case for doctors who have been sitting on the sidelines to consider buying in 2026.
