## 2. Student-loan policy (RAP / PSLF / Grad PLUS) — July 1, 2026

- **~7 weeks out** to July 1 RAP cutover.
- **RAP payments:** 1%–10% of AGI (no cap, $10/mo floor under $10K income) — confirmed across multiple second-source explainers (NerdWallet, SoFi, College Investor, SavingforCollege, StudentLoanPlanner).
- **PSLF interplay:** RAP qualifies for PSLF, but RAP payments will typically run **higher than SAVE** for attendings, meaning the forgivable balance shrinks. New borrowers on/after July 1 who want PSLF must use RAP — the standard plan does not qualify.
- **Grad PLUS:** **eliminated** for new borrowers on/after July 1, 2026. Professional students capped at **$50K/yr / $200K lifetime** in Direct Unsubsidized Loans. Med students typically borrowing $250K+ may now have to fill the gap with private loans that have **no IDR, no PSLF, no federal discharge protections**.
- **Parent PLUS:** new Parent PLUS borrowers after July 1 will have **no IDR option and no path to PSLF**. Existing borrowers wanting PSLF must consolidate into Direct Consolidation and enroll in ICR by June 30, 2026 (start by March 31 recommended) — that window has already closed for new starts in May.
- **Mortgage-side ripple:** physician lenders increasingly pre-underwriting **projected RAP payments** on the file. On a $400K student-loan balance at $500K AGI, an uncapped ~10% AGI ceiling can swing qualifying purchase price **$50K–$150K** depending on file size. Bigger swing on jumbo files.

## 3. Topic-of-the-day signal: new construction / physician construction loans

Multiple physician-finance sources (Student Loan Planner, Financial Residency, Big Law Investor, White Coat Investor, Physician on FIRE, Liberty FCU, PNC) all show a healthy 2026 market for **physician construction loans** — a niche worth a dedicated article because the rules differ meaningfully from a standard physician purchase mortgage.

Reported 2026 features and structural details:

- **Eligibility:** typically MD/DO/DPM, DDS/DMD; some lenders include CRNAs, PAs, NPs. Pharmacists are included by fewer construction-loan lenders than they are by purchase-loan lenders.
- **Down payment / LTV:** up to **100% LTV under ~$750K**, scaling down at higher loan amounts (mirrors the physician purchase ladder).
- **PMI:** none, just like the purchase product.
- **Structure — two main flavors:**
  - **One-time close (OTC) / "construction-to-perm":** one set of closing costs, rate is locked from day one; rate often slightly higher because the lender is taking 12+ months of rate risk. Synovus is named as an example of an OTC physician program.
  - **Two-time close:** separate construction loan during build, then a permanent mortgage at completion. Two sets of closing costs, two underwrites, and your **permanent-loan rate is not locked at the start** — you reprice at end-loan.
- **Draws:** funds disburse in stages tied to inspections/percent-complete.
- **Builder vetting:** lenders typically require a licensed GC with experience, contractor financial review, fixed-price contract, contingency reserve.
- **Rate-lock posture:** in a rising-rate stretch like the current 6.30 → 6.37 → ~6.33 sequence, OTC's day-one lock looks more valuable; in a falling-rate stretch, the optionality of two-close looks better.

This is genuinely underdiscussed relative to purchase loans, and physician audiences planning a build often discover the differences too late. **Today's article topic: new construction on a physician mortgage — OTC vs. two-close, the four physician-specific wrinkles, and a 2026 shopping playbook.**
