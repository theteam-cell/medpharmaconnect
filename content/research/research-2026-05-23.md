## Item 1: PSLF and Federal Student Loan Policy Changes Hitting Doctors Hard in 2026

**Source:** [PSLF is Changing in 2026 — Curbside Real Estate](https://www.curbsiderealestate.com/pslf-is-changing-in-2026/) | [Medical Economics — One Big Beautiful Bill](https://www.medicaleconomics.com/view/a-new-chapter-in-student-loans-what-the-one-big-beautiful-bill-act-means-for-physicians) | [AAMC — Proposed Changes](https://www.aamc.org/news/proposed-changes-federal-student-loans-could-worsen-doctor-shortage)

**Key Points:**
- Effective July 1, 2026: Federal Grad PLUS loans eliminated; unsubsidized Stafford loans capped at $50K/year, $200K lifetime for professional degrees
- PSLF overhaul: residency years no longer count toward the 120-payment threshold — a 5-year residency no longer earns 5 years of credit
- New Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP) replaces all existing income-driven repayment plans for new borrowers; up to 30-year term for medical school debt
- PSLF employer eligibility now explicitly allows disqualification of employers with "substantial illegal purpose" (effective July 1, 2026)
- Students already enrolled and with existing loans before July 1, 2026 have some grandfather protections

**Relevance to MedPharmaConnect:** These changes significantly alter the debt-to-income picture for residents and new attendings. Student loan burden calculations for physician mortgage DTI are directly affected. Worth addressing how mortgage lenders handle RAP payments vs. old IDR plans.

## Item 2: 2026 Physician Mortgage Rate Environment — Mid-6% to Low-7% Range

**Source:** [Physician Living — Mortgage Interest Rates 2026](https://www.physicianliving.com/articles/post/mortgage-interest-rates) | [DrLoanTool.com — Physician Mortgage Rates](https://www.drloantool.com/physician-mortgage-rates) | [PhysicianLoansUSA — 2026 Housing Outlook](https://www.physicianloansusa.com/news/2026-housing-market-outlook)

**Key Points:**
- Physician loan rates holding in the 6.25%–7.0% range in early 2026; modest declines possible by year-end
- Physician mortgages carry a small rate premium (0.125–0.375% above conventional) as the tradeoff for no PMI and flexible DTI
- Growing lender competition: more banks and non-bank lenders entering the physician loan space in 2026
- Eligibility creep: some programs now extending to CRNAs, NPs, and PAs — not just MDs/DOs
- 30-year conforming conventional rate dropped to ~6.2% nationally per NAHB

**Relevance to MedPharmaConnect:** Content opportunity — help readers understand why the rate premium is often worth it (PMI savings math). Also address expanding eligibility for non-physician medical professionals.

## Item 3: "K-Shaped" 2026 Housing Market Favors High-Income Professionals

**Source:** [NewHomeSource — K-Shaped Housing Market](https://www.newhomesource.com/news/housing-market-trends/the-k-shaped-housing-market-why-2026-will-feel-very-different/) | [Fortune — Income Growth Outpaces Home Prices](https://fortune.com/2025/12/06/housing-reset-income-growth-home-price-growth-2026-redfin/) | [JPMorgan — 2026 Housing Outlook](https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/global-research/real-estate/us-housing-market-outlook)

**Key Points:**
- Analysts describe 2026 as a "K-shaped" market: high earners re-entering first, lower/middle income households still squeezed
- National home price growth expected to stall near 0% in 2026; monthly payments declining for the first time since 2020
- Redfin forecast: income growth will outpace home price growth in 2026 — a "Great Housing Reset"
- Strong pockets: Columbus OH, Indianapolis, Kansas City outperforming; TX/FL markets cooling
- High-income households like physicians historically well-positioned: average income grew 1:1 with home prices from 1975–2024

**Relevance to MedPharmaConnect:** Physicians and high-income medical professionals are uniquely positioned to take advantage of 2026 market conditions. Could frame as "why now might be a good time to stop renting" for residents finishing training.
