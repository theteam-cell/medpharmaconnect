# Research Notes — 2026-04-24

Three items picked up from today's scan relevant to MedPharmaConnect's audience (physicians, dentists, pharmacists, residents) and physician mortgage shopping.

## 1. Physician mortgage rates holding in the 6.5%–7.0% range; lender-to-lender spread is wide

Current physician mortgage rates are sitting in the roughly 6.5%–7.0% range for 30-year fixed, running about 0.125%–0.50% above comparable conventional rates. The broader 30-year conventional average on April 23, 2026 was 6.12%, with 15-year around 5.50%. Projections for the rest of 2026 keep physician mortgage rates in the 6.25%–7.0% band with only modest declines possible by year-end.

The most actionable takeaway for borrowers: because physician mortgages are portfolio products, the spread between lenders is consistently wider than for conventional loans. Shopping 3–5 lenders can reveal 0.25%–0.50% rate differences on the same file — real money over 30 years. Worth reinforcing the "always shop at least three" message in content.

## 2. 2026 housing market: income growth is forecast to outpace home-price growth for the first time in years

Redfin, Realtor.com, and Zillow are all pointing at the same setup for 2026: incomes growing ~3.6%, inflation ~3%, and median home-sale prices rising only about 1% year over year. Inventory is forecast to rise roughly 10%. In plain English, affordability is finally improving — slowly — even if rates stay in the 6s. The split is regional: Northeast and West Coast stay tight and competitive; Midwest and South have more inventory and softer price growth.

The relevant angle for physicians and dentists: income growth works in their favor harder than in the general market. An attending stepping up from resident pay (or a dentist moving from associate to partner) already has fast income growth relative to the national 3.6% average, which compounds the affordability improvement. This is a good "it might be a reasonable year to buy" data point — without overselling it.

## 3. July 1, 2026 student-loan changes are ~9 weeks out — and the residency PSLF rule shift is the big one

The July 1, 2026 cutoff is now roughly nine weeks away. Two changes matter for our audience:

- **Residency and fellowship years will no longer count toward PSLF** for loans borrowed on or after July 1, 2026. For a 5-year residency, that's 5 lost payment-count years, pushing forgiveness deep into the attending career.
- **Grad PLUS loans are being eliminated.** Federal Direct Unsubsidized caps at $50K/year and $200K total. New med, dental, and pharmacy students starting in fall 2026 will have to cover the gap with private loans or institutional aid.

Important nuance for content: borrowers with loans **originated before July 1, 2026** and at qualifying employers are grandfathered under existing rules — their residency payments still count. This distinction matters because most current residents and attendings reading our content already have pre-cutoff loans and shouldn't panic. The AAMC and AMA are both warning the changes could worsen physician shortages.

Tie-in to physician mortgages: the IDR-payment-counts-as-DTI angle remains the same — lenders using the documented IDR payment will continue to give residents and early-career attendings a real qualifying advantage on the mortgage side, regardless of what happens on the PSLF side.
