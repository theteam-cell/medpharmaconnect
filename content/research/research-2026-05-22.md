## Topic 1: Physician Mortgage Rate Outlook for 2026

**Source:** Physician Living, Physician Loans USA, WealthKeel, DrLoanTool.com  
**Key Findings:**
- 30-year fixed physician mortgage rates are running in the mid-6% to low-7% range in early 2026
- Physician loans carry a 0.125%–0.50% rate premium (the "doctor tax") above conventional mortgages
- That premium adds roughly $25,000–$30,000 in extra interest on a $500,000 loan over 30 years
- Top economists characterize 2026 as an improving opportunity: rising home inventory and modest rate declines expected by year-end
- Shopping 3–5 lenders can reveal rate differences of 0.25%–0.50%, potentially saving thousands annually
- Programs with 0% down and no PMI remain widely available for qualifying medical professionals

**Relevance for MedPharmaConnect:** Good basis for articles on rate shopping strategy, the true cost of physician loans vs. conventional, and timing a home purchase in 2026.

## Topic 2: Federal Student Loan Policy Changes Affecting Physicians (Effective July 1, 2026)

**Source:** AAMC, CNBC, Medical Economics, Money.com  
**Key Findings:**
- The "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" significantly restructures federal student lending for new borrowers starting July 1, 2026
- Federal Direct Unsubsidized Loans capped at $50,000/year and $200,000 lifetime for graduate/professional programs
- Federal Grad PLUS Program eliminated for new borrowers — a major blow since it was the primary funding vehicle for MD/DO/DDS programs
- New repayment options streamlined to two plans: Tiered Standard Plan (10–25 year fixed) and Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP, income-driven, up to 30 years)
- PSLF still exists, but residency years will no longer count toward the 10-year forgiveness clock for new borrowers
- Students who borrowed before July 1, 2026 have a transitional window and can continue under old rules for up to 3 academic years
- Impact: Future medical graduates may carry more private debt, making physician mortgage programs (which exclude student debt from DTI calculations) even more strategically important

**Relevance for MedPharmaConnect:** High-priority topic — these changes directly affect the financial calculus for future residents/attendings considering home purchases. The DTI exclusion of student loans in physician mortgages becomes even more valuable.

## Topic 3: 2026 Housing Market for High-Income Buyers

**Source:** HousingWire, Fortune, Compass, Robb Report  
**Key Findings:**
- Compass forecasts U.S. housing market stabilization in 2026: prices essentially flat, inventory up ~5%, sales up 4–5%
- Spring 2026 is showing a "thaw" after 3 years of a frozen market — more listings are coming online
- Luxury/high-end segment ($1.3M+ nationally) remains resilient; high-income buyers less sensitive to rate movements
- Roughly $6 trillion in global wealth transferred in 2025 alone, creating a new wave of well-capitalized buyers
- Affordability concerns are more pronounced at the low end of the market; the physician income tier sits above most affordability headwinds
- Multi-generational living is a rising trend (~1 in 5 luxury purchases) — relevant for physicians with aging parents or extended families

**Relevance for MedPharmaConnect:** Reinforces the case for physicians to act in 2026 rather than waiting, given improving inventory and stabilizing prices. Also supports articles on home purchase timing relative to career stage.
