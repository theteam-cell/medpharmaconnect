# Research Notes — 2026-04-25

## 1. Physician mortgage rate environment held in mid-6% range with wide lender spreads

Current 30-year fixed physician mortgage rates are running in the **6.5%–7.0% range** for most borrowers, with industry trackers suggesting rates will stay in the **6.25%–7.0% band** for the rest of 2026. The "doctor tax" — the rate premium over comparable conventional loans — is consistently cited at **0.125%–0.50%**, which works out to roughly $25,000–$30,000 in extra interest over 30 years on a $500,000 loan.

Important shopping note repeated across sources: physician mortgages are **portfolio products**, so the same borrower can get materially different quotes from different lenders. The recommendation is consistent — **get at least three quotes**. The spread between physician lenders is wider than the spread between conventional lenders, so shopping pays more for physician loans than for any other loan type.

## 2. July 1, 2026 federal student loan changes — ~9 weeks out

The OBBBA (One Big Beautiful Bill Act) changes take effect **July 1, 2026**. Headline impacts for medical professionals:

- **Grad PLUS eliminated for new borrowers.** Professional students get capped at **$50,000/year** in federal Unsubsidized Loans, **$200,000 lifetime**. Anything above that has to come from credit-based private loans.
- **Existing borrowers get a grandfather window.** Students who already took a Federal Direct Loan before July 1, 2026 can keep borrowing through Grad PLUS until graduation or for up to **3 academic years**, whichever comes first.
- **Repayment plans collapse to two:** Tiered Standard and the new Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP), which becomes the only IDR option for new borrowers.
- **PSLF still applies to residency/internship** — earlier draft language that would have excluded training years was dropped.

Practical implication for the physician mortgage audience: residents and fellows on existing IDR plans who plan to keep using documented IDR payments to qualify for a physician mortgage should **lock down their current repayment plan documentation now**, before any administrative chaos hits in July.

## 3. Spring 2026 housing market — most balanced in a decade, first-timers returning

Mortgage rates climbed from 6.22% on March 19 to **6.57% by mid-spring 2026** — the highest since early 2024 — but the market itself is rebalancing. Three signals worth noting:

- **First-time buyers were 34% of February purchases**, exceeding the prior-year annual share. Re-entry of first-time buyers is a leading indicator of market health.
- **Affordability is mathematically improving** because incomes are growing while monthly payments are flat-to-down. This is a structural shift; for the past five years home-price growth outpaced wage growth.
- **Regional divergence is sharp.** Austin (-15%) and Miami (-17%) show falling sales; Midwestern markets like Columbus, Indianapolis, and Kansas City are showing pockets of strength. High-income physician buyers in coastal markets face very different math than peers in the Midwest.

For the MedPharmaConnect audience, the takeaway is that the spring 2026 market gives buyers more leverage than they've had since 2019 — and physicians who've been waiting on the sidelines for "rates to drop" may be better served by the inventory and negotiating dynamics available right now.
