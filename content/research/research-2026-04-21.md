# Research Notes — 2026-04-21

## Item 1: 30-year fixed mortgage rates continue their slow thaw

As of April 20, 2026, the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate is running around 6.30% (U.S. News daily tracker), consistent with Freddie Mac's most recent PMMS print and a few basis points below last week. The broader consensus from Fannie Mae's March 2026 forecast remains that conventional rates will drift under 6% by the end of the year, with a year-end target around 5.7%. Physician-loan pricing — because it is portfolio-priced and carries a typical 0.125%–0.50% "doctor tax" above the agency market — is currently sitting in the 6.25%–7.0% range, with the bottom of that band accessible only to the cleanest borrower profiles (high FICO, low DTI, large reserves).

**Why it matters for MedPharmaConnect readers:** The rate conversation has shifted from "should I wait?" to "where am I on the rate distribution?" A physician borrower getting quoted 7.0% in April 2026 is probably paying for lender friction or a weak file, not market conditions. Rate shopping is the leverage point, not timing.

Source: U.S. News Money — "Today's Mortgage Rates Continue to Fall: April 20, 2026"; Freddie Mac PMMS; Fannie Mae March 2026 Economic & Housing Outlook.

## Item 2: Dentist-specific physician mortgage programs are quietly expanding

Updated 2026 lender roundups show several meaningful expansions in DDS/DMD-eligible physician mortgage programs this spring. Fifth Third now advertises up to $1.25M at 0% down for residents/interns and up to $2.5M with low down payments for established dentists. BMO's physician program offers dentists 0% down up to $1M and 5% down up to $1.5M in most states (excluding NY). TD Bank's Medical Professional Mortgage remains open to dentists within 10 years of residency. Truist, First Citizens, and Physician Bank all carry dedicated dentist tracks. The practical effect: dentists no longer have to treat themselves as an afterthought on "physician mortgage" pages — there are meaningful program differences worth shopping.

**Why it matters:** Dentists have historically been underserved in our content, despite being one of MedPharmaConnect's named audiences. A solid dentist-focused piece is overdue — especially one that calls out where the product *differs* for dentists (practice-loan interactions, DSO employment vs. private practice, shorter training timeline than MDs).

Source: Financial Residency — "11 Best Mortgages for Dentists in 2026"; Student Loan Planner — "DDS / DMD Mortgage"; Physician Bank Dentist Home Loan page; Truist Doctor Loan.

## Item 3: July 1, 2026 federal student loan changes will reshape physician home-buying math

Two scheduled changes hit on July 1, 2026, and both have real implications for physician mortgage underwriting:

1. **Residency and fellowship years will no longer count toward PSLF** for loans borrowed on or after that date. For an incoming M1, that means the PSLF clock effectively doesn't start until attendinghood — pushing a five-year PSLF timeline from roughly year 8 of training to year 13.
2. **Graduate PLUS loans are being eliminated**, with future borrowers capped at $50K/year in unsubsidized Stafford and a lifetime cap of $200K for professional degrees. That cap does not come close to covering the typical $250K–$400K cost of medical or dental school. Expect heavier reliance on private loans, which have different DTI treatment in underwriting (no IDR carve-out, no deferment exclusion).

**Why it matters:** The physician mortgage product was designed around a specific federal student loan landscape (IDR payments, PSLF, generous deferment rules). If a meaningful share of future medical/dental students carry private rather than federal debt, the flexible DTI treatment that makes the physician mortgage so powerful becomes less useful. This is not a 2026 homebuyer problem — but it is a 2026 *content* opportunity to help readers understand how the pipeline is shifting. Current residents and attendings with pre-2026-07-01 federal loans are unaffected.

Source: AAMC — "Proposed changes to federal student loans could worsen the doctor shortage"; MD Preferred Network — "Physician Student Loan Forgiveness in 2026"; Panacea Financial — "Federal Student Loan Updates."
