# Research Notes — 2026-05-05

## 1) Physician mortgage rates holding mid-6% to low-7% as Basel III spreads stick
30-yr fixed physician mortgages remain mid-6% to low-7% in early May, sitting
roughly 0.125%–0.50% above comparable conventional rates and showing 0.25%–0.50%
lender-to-lender dispersion on identical files. If conventional rates ease to
~6.0% by late 2026, physician fixed rates likely settle 6.25%–6.50% — but only
if bank liquidity and inflation expectations cooperate. Several sources flag that
Basel III phase-ins may *widen* the physician-vs-conventional spread further if
banks pull back on portfolio balance-sheet exposure. Physician ARMs continue to
price 0.50%–1.00% below 30-yr fixed, the widest gap of the cycle, because
rate-shy buyers are clustering into fixed and pushing the short end down.

Implication: shop ≥3 quotes inside a 14-day window, and treat the rate gap
between best/worst quote on the same file as the real opportunity, not the
headline national average.

## 2) Lender treatment of RVU/bonus income tightening — base salary still the anchor
Multiple physician-mortgage shops are emphasizing that **only guaranteed base
salary** counts toward qualification when a contract has a meaningful RVU or
production component. Bonus, RVU, sign-on, and moonlighting income generally
require a 2-year documented history before underwriters will average it into
qualifying income (typically averaged across 24 months of W-2/1099/tax returns).
For physicians with **pure RVU contracts** (e.g., some hospitalist, surgical,
anesthesia, radiology, pathology setups), several physician-loan banks are
asking for 2 years of tax returns before pre-approving — effectively excluding
new attendings on RVU-only contracts from the standard "employment-contract-only"
underwriting path that's the headline draw of the physician mortgage.

Implication: doctors with bonus/RVU/partnership income should ask each lender
*explicitly* how they qualify on base vs. variable income, and shop the lenders
who count more of the variable comp — the dollar difference at the DTI line
can be $50K–$150K of additional purchasing power.

## 3) RAP / PSLF transition now ~8 weeks out — July 1, 2026 cutover
The Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP) is mandated to be in effect no later than
July 1, 2026. Confirmed mechanics:
- RAP payment is 1%–10% of AGI (flat $10/mo if AGI < $10K), with no cap.
- For loans first taken out on/after July 1, 2026, the only options are RAP or
  the Standard plan. Standard does **not** qualify for PSLF, so PSLF-eligible
  new borrowers must enroll in RAP.
- Existing borrowers (no new loans after July 1, 2026) may stay in IBR,
  Graduated, Extended, or Standard — or switch to RAP — through July 1, 2028.
  After that date, un-opted borrowers will be auto-enrolled in IBR or RAP.
- ED is set to start issuing notices to SAVE-plan borrowers to exit and
  re-enroll in a legal plan within 90 days.

Implication for mortgage qualification: physician-loan underwriters are
increasingly **pre-underwriting dual DTIs** — current PAYE/IBR vs. projected RAP
— to stress-test approval. AGI-based RAP payments at 1%–10% of attending AGI
can run materially higher than current PAYE/IBR for many residents and new
attendings, particularly two-physician households. This can push a borderline
DTI over the limit even on a physician-mortgage product.
