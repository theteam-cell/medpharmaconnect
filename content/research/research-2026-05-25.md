## Topic 1: Physician Mortgage Rate Landscape in 2026

**Source:** PhysicianLiving.com, PhysicianLoansUSA.com, WealthKeel.com

- 30-year fixed physician mortgage rates are currently in the **mid-6% to low-7% range**, varying by LTV, credit score, and loan amount.
- Physician loans carry a **0.125%–0.50% rate premium** ("doctor tax") over conventional mortgages — roughly $25,000–$30,000 in extra interest on a $500K loan over 30 years.
- An increasing number of lenders are entering the physician loan space, making this a competitive window to shop rates.
- Shopping across 3–5 lenders can reveal differences of 0.25%–0.50%, potentially saving thousands annually.
- Zero-down and no-PMI benefits remain core features of physician loan programs in 2026.

## Topic 2: Major Student Loan Policy Changes Affecting Doctors (July 1, 2026)

**Source:** CurbsideRealEstate.com, AAMC students-residents.aamc.org, ThemdPreferredNetwork.com

- **PSLF changes effective July 1, 2026:** Medical residencies will no longer count as qualifying public service employment for borrowers who did NOT take out Direct PLUS or Unsubsidized Loans by June 30, 2025.
- Physicians finishing a 5-year residency after this cutoff lose 5 years of PSLF credit — pushing forgiveness timelines significantly further into their attending career.
- **Grad PLUS loans eliminated** for new borrowers starting July 1, 2026.
- Federal Direct Unsubsidized Loans capped at **$50,000/year and $200,000 lifetime** for new borrowers.
- Repayment options consolidated into two plans: Tiered Standard Plan and Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP).
- **Key implication for home buying:** Physicians facing loan policy uncertainty may look to buy homes sooner rather than later, or may need to reassess their debt-to-income picture under new repayment plans.
- Physician mortgage programs continue to handle student loan debt flexibly — often excluding or reducing student loan payments in DTI calculations.

## Topic 3: Spring 2026 Housing Market — K-Shaped Dynamics for High Earners

**Source:** NewHomeSource.com, Ramsey Solutions, NAR.realtor

- Home prices forecast to reach **$419,000–$427,000** nationally in 2026, roughly 2–3% growth over 2025 — broadly in line with inflation.
- Mortgage rates edging down toward (and in some cases below) 6%, with 15-year fixed rates averaging around 5.2% — first meaningful affordability improvement since 2020.
- **"K-shaped" housing market:** High-income earners are in a strong position to reenter the market but some analysts suggest the buy-vs.-rent math still favors renting in many markets for those who can invest the difference.
- Mortgage applications surged 47% from January to April 2026, reflecting seasonal spring demand — pipeline of buyers is active.
- For physician buyers, improving affordability + more lender competition in physician loan space = favorable window in spring/summer 2026.
