# Research Notes — 2026-05-16

## 1. Mortgage rates eased fractionally this week
- Freddie Mac PMMS for the week ending May 14, 2026: **30-yr fixed averaged 6.36%**, down 1 bp from 6.37% the prior week. A year ago (mid-May 2025) the 30-yr FRM averaged 6.81%, so the headline rate sits ~45 bp below spring-2025 levels.
- **15-yr fixed: 5.71%** (down 1 bp from 5.72%).
- This breaks the two-week uptick noted on May 11–13 and brings the survey rate back near the YTD baseline. Daily indices through May 14 show intra-week volatility in the 6.30%–6.51% range, so the smoothed weekly print understates how jumpy short-term moves have been.
- May–July consensus forecasts still cluster **5.9%–6.5%**, with practical refi threshold for spring-2024/2025 originations not expected before late-2026 / early-2027. CBS News' May 2026 expert panel was split between "drift down to high-5s by year-end" and "stuck in mid-6s on sticky inflation."

## 2. Physician-loan band unchanged but rate-lock mechanics are the lever this week
- Physician 30-yr fixed still mid-6% to low-7%, with the familiar **0.125%–0.50% premium over conventional** and **0.25%–0.50% lender dispersion** on identical files. Bankrate, LeverageRx, WealthKeel, and SalaryDr 2026 best-of lists all converge on the same band; no headline product changes this week.
- What changed: with two weeks of choppy daily prints, multiple physician-mortgage shops (AmeriSave, Pegasus Lends, Mo The Broker) are pushing **rate-lock and float-down strategy** harder than in April. Practical takeaways from the May 2026 lender writeups:
  - **30- and 45-day locks** remain free at most banks; **60-, 75-, 90-day** locks cost **0.125–0.375 discount points** in 2026 — slightly wider than 2024's 0.125–0.25.
  - **Float-down options** typically cost **0.25–0.50 points** and trigger if market rates drop **≥ 0.25%** before closing. Some lenders cap to one float-down per file.
  - For new construction and complex condos, 60–90-day locks plus a written extension-fee schedule are now standard physician-loan advice.
  - The Yahoo Personal Finance and Bankrate May 1 takes both note that float-down is more interesting than usual right now because the curve could come down through summer, but the float-down only earns out if the rate move clears the up-front 0.25–0.50 points.

## 3. SAVE wind-down clock — ~6.5 weeks to July 1
- Department of Education has formally directed the **~7.5 million SAVE borrowers** to exit and select a legal repayment plan; servicers begin issuing **90-day transition notices on July 1, 2026**.
- **RAP launches July 1, 2026** alongside the new **Tiered Standard Plan**. RAP is AGI-based (not discretionary income), with a **$10/mo minimum** and effective rate scaling roughly **1%–10% of AGI** (no payment cap for high earners).
- CRS analysis (IF13075) and Medical Economics' May "10 things every physician needs to know before July 1" both emphasize that **professional-degree borrowers with high DTI may pay more lifetime under RAP** than they would have under SAVE/PAYE, because they're unlikely to reach forgiveness inside the standard window.
- Implication for mortgages: lenders are increasingly **pre-underwriting projected RAP payments** for any file that closes after July 1. For physician borrowers with $200K+ federal student debt and AGI > $400K, RAP-based DTI can be $1,500–$4,000/mo higher than current SAVE/PAYE — large enough to swing qualifying purchase price by **$50K–$150K** on identical files.

## Article angle for today
Rate-lock strategy for physician mortgages in spring 2026. Three reasons to write it now: (a) two weeks of daily-rate volatility have made lock timing a real decision; (b) most physician borrowers don't know lock-period premiums and float-down economics; (c) longer locks (60–90 days) interact directly with the July 1 RAP cutover for any file that won't fund until late June or after. Frame: how lock periods are priced, when float-down earns out, and the four physician-specific lock-timing situations.
