# Research Notes — 2026-06-03

## 1. Physician mortgage rates holding in the mid-6% range
30-year fixed physician loans are sitting roughly 6.5%–7.0%, with forecasts for only modest declines through year-end 2026 (possibly dipping just below 6% by late 2026 per Fannie Mae). Physician loans still carry a ~0.125%–0.50% "doctor tax" premium over comparable conventional rates — on a $500K loan that's roughly $25K–$30K extra interest over 30 years. Takeaway for our audience: shop at least three quotes; the lender spread is wider than on conventional loans.
- Source: https://www.drloantool.com/physician-mortgage-rates
- Source: https://www.physicianloansusa.com/news/2026-housing-market-outlook

## 2. Student loan overhaul effective July 1, 2026 (well-covered already on site)
Grad PLUS ends for new borrowers; Direct Unsubsidized capped at $50K/yr, $200K total. New borrowers funnel into a Tiered Standard Plan or the Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP) — the only IDR and only PSLF-eligible plan going forward, payments 1%–10% of income, up to 30-year term, with unpaid-interest relief and a $50/mo principal reduction. Current med students who borrowed before July 1 get grandfathering (Grad PLUS / older limits until graduation or up to 3 years). Note: site already has 5+ articles on this — avoid re-treading.
- Source: https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/29/student-loan-borrowers-new-repayment-plans.html
- Source: https://www.studentloanplanner.com/medical-residency-rap-payment/

## 3. 2026 housing market: inventory thawing, prices up modestly
Inventory ~20% above a year ago but still below pre-COVID normal. Rates expected stable in low-to-mid 6% through summer. Affordability has improved 8 straight months as income growth outpaces price gains — a tailwind that disproportionately helps high-income buyers. Fannie Mae projects home prices +3.2% in 2026, +1.9% in 2027.
