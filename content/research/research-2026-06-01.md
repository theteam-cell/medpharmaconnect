# Research Notes — 2026-06-01

## Theme of the day: Locum tenens / 1099 physicians and home financing

### 1. Locum tenens is now mainstream — and growing
- The percentage of physicians currently working locum tenens has nearly **tripled in a decade, from 5% to 14%**. An estimated **56,000 physicians** now work locums.
- **41%** of physicians report having worked locums at some point (up from 20% in 2016).
- Market size projected to grow from **$9.1B (2024) to $9.9B (2026)**, ~4–5% annual growth.
- Drivers: burnout (55% report debilitating stress), desire for schedule control, autonomy, higher daily pay.
- In-demand specialties command **$2,500–$3,500/day** (psychiatry, hospitalist, EM, anesthesiology).
- Source: Aequor, Weatherby 2026 Locum Tenens Physician Report (HIT Consultant), Sermo.

### 2. How locum/1099 physicians qualify for a physician mortgage
- Conventional underwriting is hard on 1099 income — usually wants **2 years of tax returns** and averages net (post-deduction) income, which understates take-home.
- Physician mortgage programs are more flexible: some allow qualifying on a **signed employment/locum contract** with a guaranteed rate + stated hours, plus a letter confirming no expenses to the doctor.
- Physician loans still offer **0% down, no PMI**, and use **IBR/actual student-loan payment** for DTI instead of a percentage-of-balance.
- Qualified borrowers (700+ credit) can access **up to $1M with 0% down**.
- Watch-out: aggressive business deductions (QBI etc.) lower taxable income and can shrink the income a conventional lender will count.
- Sources: Student Loan Planner, Physician Side Gigs, Medical Professional Home Loans.

### 3. Rate + market backdrop (June 2026)
- 30-year fixed averaging **~6.56%** as of June 1, 2026 (6.53% late May).
- Forecast: rates expected to hover **6.0%–6.4%** June 2026 through May 2027.
- Inventory still tight; home prices projected up **2%–3%** for 2026–2027.
- Best pricing favors 740+ credit. ARMs more popular on jumbo/nonconforming loans.
- Sources: CBS News, Norada, Freddie Mac PMMS, Bankrate.
