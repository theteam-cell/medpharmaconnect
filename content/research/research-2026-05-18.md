## 2. Pharmacist eligibility on physician-mortgage programs (2026)

- PharmDs **are** included in many physician-mortgage programs alongside MD/DO/DDS/DMD/DPM/DVM/CRNA — but **not all banks extend pharmacist eligibility**, and several banks cap pharmacist down-payment tiers more conservatively than MD tiers.
- Active 2026 PharmD-eligible programs include: First Merchants Bank, Flagstar Bank, UMB Bank, Fulton Bank, Liberty FCU, Physician Bank, and several regional banks/credit unions.
- Common pharmacist terms: 0% down typically capped at ~$500K–$750K (vs. ~$1M for MDs at the same lender), 5–10% down up to $1M–$1.5M, no PMI throughout, jumbo ladders shorter than for MDs (rarely above $1.5M–$2M).
- **Eligibility-by-bank is the #1 confirmation point** — pharmacists who assume MD eligibility = pharmacist eligibility waste credit pulls.

## 3. PharmD debt + income profile (relevant to DTI math)

- Average PharmD graduating debt: **~$167,711** (AACP / 2023 data, modest downward drift from 2020 peak of $179,514).
- Public school average: ~$143,338. Private school average: ~$204,576.
- **82% of PharmDs borrow** to finish school.
- PGY1 residency stipends: $58K–$75K; PGY2: $60K–$92K — but unlike MDs, **only a minority of pharmacists complete residency**; most enter retail/hospital practice at $125K–$155K out of school.
- Implication: pharmacists usually qualify on **W-2 income, not future-contract** treatment (no "resident → attending" letter trick), and the salary ceiling sits materially lower than MD/DO/DDS — debt-to-income headroom is tighter even with no PMI.

## 4. RAP / student-loan cutover (July 1, 2026 — now ~6 weeks out)

- SAVE / PAYE / ICR phasing out for new borrowers immediately; existing borrowers transition between July 2026 and July 2028.
- **Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP):** 1%–10% of full AGI (no discretionary-income carve-out), uncapped, $10/mo minimum, 30-year forgiveness, professional-student caps $50K/yr / $200K total for new borrowers.
- **Critical pharmacist wrinkle:** taking **any** new federal student loan after July 1, 2026 forces ALL prior loans into RAP, eliminating SAVE/PAYE grandfathering.
- DTI impact on $150K pharmacist AGI: projected RAP at 6%–8% tier ≈ $750–$1,000/mo — meaningfully higher than SAVE/PAYE on the same balance. Physician-mortgage lenders now pre-underwriting projected RAP for any file closing after July 1.
