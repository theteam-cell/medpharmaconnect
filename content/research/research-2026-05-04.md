## 2. Physician-loan ARM pricing widens vs. fixed — 50–100 bp discount on 5/6 and 7/6 ARMs
- Multiple lender reviews confirm physician ARMs are pricing 0.50%–1.00% below the comparable 30-year fixed in spring 2026, the widest discount we've seen in this cycle.
- Typical structure: 30-day average SOFR index + ~3.50% margin. 5/6 ARM has 2/1/5 caps; 7/6 and 10/6 ARMs have 5/1/5 caps.
- Concrete number being quoted: on a $500,000 loan, a 5/6 ARM vs. 30-year fixed saves ~$200–$400/month during the fixed period (~$12K–$24K over 5 years).
- This widening is partly because rate-shy buyers are clustering into 30-year fixed, leaving lenders willing to discount ARMs more aggressively — and partly because the inverted/flattening curve has come down on the short end.
- Risk to highlight: the cap structure matters more than the headline discount. A 5/6 ARM with 2/1/5 caps means the rate can jump up to 2 percentage points at first reset, then 1 point per period, with a 5-point lifetime cap. That's a meaningful payment shock at PGY-7 if you don't move or refi.

## 3. RAP rollout July 1 — now ~8 weeks out; payment-shock math vs. PAYE/IBR is real
- For new medical-student borrowers, the federal repayment menu collapses to a Tiered Standard Plan and the Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP) as the only IDR option. Up to a 30-year repayment term for medical-school borrowers under RAP.
- RAP pays 1–10% of adjusted gross income (AGI), not discretionary income — meaningfully higher denominator than PAYE/IBR for a high-AGI attending. $10/month minimum. 30-year forgiveness window. Includes interest-relief features (unpaid interest forgiveness, $50/month principal reduction when payment doesn't cover interest).
- Existing borrowers retain PAYE/IBR/SAVE-replacement options for now, but PSLF mechanics around residency credit and employer eligibility are tightening.
- Mortgage relevance: lenders are increasingly running dual DTIs — what current PAYE looks like and what the RAP-equivalent payment would look like — to stress-test approval. Borrowers who plan to be on RAP within 2026 should ask for both numbers up front.
