## Item 1: Non-Bank Lenders Are Reshaping the Physician Mortgage Market

**Source:** National Mortgage News; DrLoanTool.com; physicianliving.com  
**Relevance:** High — directly affects the product landscape physicians encounter when shopping

Key findings:
- **Redwood Trust** issued its first RMBS for medical professionals on March 26, 2026 — a **$482 million** deal comprising 607 loans with an average credit score of 769. This signals the physician mortgage product is now entering prime private-label securitization channels at scale.
- **Newrez** (the lending unit of Rithm Capital) launched its **Medical Professional Home Loan** in 2026 — 100% financing, no traditional PMI, flexible DTI treatment, available for purchases and rate-and-term refinances on primary residences, condos, and co-ops. The program is now available through the wholesale (broker) channel.
- KBRA estimates medical professional mortgage production entering prime private-label channels could reach **$5 billion annually**.
- The physician mortgage space is shifting from an exclusively bank-portfolio product to one with active non-bank/broker competition. Implication: broader availability, wider price dispersion, and more shopping leverage for borrowers.
- Physician loan rates mid-6% to low-7%, with 0.125%–0.50% premium over conventional and 0.25%–0.50% lender dispersion on identical files — that dispersion is now wider due to non-bank entrants.

## Item 2: 2026 Housing Market Window for Physician Buyers

**Source:** Curbside Real Estate; PhysicianLoansUSA; 1st Source; multiple housing market forecasts  
**Relevance:** High — supports or undermines the case for buying now

Key findings:
- 30-year fixed conventional mortgage currently hovering around **6%**, with forecasts for 2026 average around 6.0%–6.3%. Physician loan band runs approximately 0.125%–0.50% higher.
- Top economists describe 2026 as a **window of opportunity**: lower mortgage rates and rising supply expected to open the housing market after years of stagnation.
- Existing home inventory **+8.1% YoY**, months of supply ~4.1 — highest since 2019 in many metros. ~35% of sellers giving up sub-5% mortgage rates to list, making the lock-in effect less dominant.
- National home price growth projected at **0%–3% in 2026** vs. ~3.6% wage growth — first year in several where income growth outpaces home-price growth.
- ~80% of buyers are no longer waiting for rates to drop; 43% of agents report more activity than spring 2025.
- For physician buyers specifically: 0% down, no PMI, and flexible student debt treatment remain powerful entry levers in a stabilizing but still-competitive market.

## Item 3: July 1, 2026 Student Loan Policy Changes — Final ~5 Weeks Out

**Source:** AAMC; Panacea Financial; Credible; SERMO; StudentLoanPlanner  
**Relevance:** Very high — affects physician mortgage DTI calculations for any file closing on or after July 1

Key findings:
- **Grad PLUS loans eliminated** for new borrowers starting July 1, 2026. New federal borrowing cap of **$50,000/year / $200,000 lifetime** for professional degrees. Median medical school cost of attendance: $297,745 (public) to $408,150 (private) — far exceeding new caps.
- **PSLF tightening**: Medical residencies will no longer count as public service jobs for PSLF purposes for borrowers who did not take out qualifying federal loans by June 30, 2025.
- **New Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP)** launches July 1 — sole IDR option for new borrowers. Payments 1%–10% of AGI with no cap; SAVE and ICR expected to be eliminated by July 2028.
- Physicians currently in repayment largely grandfathered under existing rules, but lenders are **increasingly pre-underwriting projected RAP payments** for any file closing after July 1 — this can shift qualifying purchase price by $50K–$150K on high-AGI, high-debt files.
- **Takeaway for mortgage applicants:** A pre-approval secured before July 1 may use more favorable student-loan DTI treatment. Borrowers expecting to close after July 1 should ask lenders explicitly how they will handle RAP.
