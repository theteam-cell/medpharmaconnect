# Research Notes — May 7, 2026

## 1. Physician mortgage rates holding in mid-6% to low-7% range
- Physician loan rates currently sit roughly between 6.5% and 7.0% for 30-year fixed products in early-to-mid 2026.
- The "doctor tax" premium over conventional mortgages remains in the 0.125%–0.50% range, since physician loans are portfolio products held on lender balance sheets.
- Fannie Mae expects conventional 30-year rates to potentially dip just below 6.0% by late 2026; physician loan rates likely follow but stay slightly elevated.
- Shopping 3–5 lenders can yield 0.25%–0.50% rate spreads — meaningful savings on jumbo balances.
- Source: Physician Living, WealthKeel, DrLoanTool, SalaryDr.

## 2. Major federal student loan changes hit July 1, 2026
- Graduate PLUS program eliminated for new borrowers starting July 1, 2026.
- New federal cap for professional students: $50,000/year, $200,000 lifetime.
- Many med, dental, and pharmacy students will need significant private financing to fill the gap (estimates of $200K+ in private debt for some programs).
- PSLF rules tightening: Department of Education gains authority to disqualify certain employers; residency/fellowship years may no longer count toward PSLF in some structures.
- Implication for home buying: existing borrowers on IDR plans should lock in physician mortgage approvals while current DTI treatment is favorable; new borrowers entering training in 2026+ will face a different debt mix that lenders are still figuring out how to underwrite.
- Sources: Curbside Real Estate, AAMC, Detroit News, White Coat Investor, Panacea Financial.

## 3. 2026 housing market: rates stabilizing, prices flat-to-modestly-up
- 30-year conventional mortgage rates expected to stabilize in the low-to-mid 6% range, around 6.3% on average.
- US home price growth projected at 0%–3% nationally in 2026 — well below wage growth (~3.6%) and roughly in line with inflation (~3%).
- Net effect: affordability is slowly improving for high-income earners as wages outpace home price gains.
- A notable trend: more high-income professionals choosing to rent for flexibility ("lifestyle renters"), particularly relevant to physicians weighing mobility during early career.
- Sources: J.P. Morgan, Money.com, NAR, Yahoo Finance, HousingWire.

## Implications for MedPharmaConnect audience
- Timing matters: borrowers eligible for physician mortgages under current IDR-friendly underwriting should not wait passively for rates to drop — the policy environment is shifting faster than the rate environment.
- Education angle: many doctors, dentists, and pharmacists do not yet realize how the July 1, 2026 changes affect them. Strong content opportunity.
- Continue emphasizing: physician loans are tools, not deals — fit, fees, and lender quality matter more than the headline rate.
