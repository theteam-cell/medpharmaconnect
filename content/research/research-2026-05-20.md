## 2. Student-loan policy (RAP / PSLF) — July 1, 2026

- **~6 weeks out** to the July 1 RAP cutover.
- **RAP mechanics (confirmed across NerdWallet, SoFi, Fidelity, College Investor, SavingforCollege, Congress.gov CRS):** payments run **1%–10% of AGI**, **$10/mo minimum**, and **each eligible dependent reduces the payment by $50**. Forgiveness takes **30 years** regardless of undergrad vs. graduate debt. RAP includes **interest protection** — borrowers making full on-time payments are shielded from runaway interest and a **$50/mo unpaid-principal match** keeps the balance moving down.
- **Plan availability:** only **Direct Loan** borrowers are eligible (Parent PLUS excluded). For anyone borrowing **on or after July 1, 2026**, RAP is the **only IDR plan**. Current borrowers who take no new loans may stay on their existing IDR plan for now. **ICR and PAYE end by July 1, 2028** — holdouts get auto-moved to RAP (or IBR if RAP-ineligible).
- **Mortgage-side ripple:** physician lenders continue to **pre-underwrite projected RAP payments** on any file closing after July 1. Because RAP's percentage-of-AGI payment is uncapped, the qualifying-DTI swing is largest on high-AGI, high-balance files.

## 3. Topic-of-the-day signal: credit score on a physician mortgage

Physician-finance and credit sources (SalaryDr, Student Loan Planner, White Coat Investor, Bankrate, Experian, myFICO) point to **credit score** as an underdiscussed lever on the physician product — most coverage focuses on student-debt DTI and assumes the borrower's credit is fine. Worth a dedicated article.

Reported 2026 details:

- **Minimum scores:** physician programs generally want **700–720**, though the band across lenders runs **660–720**. Some lenders (e.g., First Horizon) accept scores as low as **~670**. The lowest tier qualifies you for the *program* but not the best *pricing*.
- **Score → terms ladder:** the most generous structures key off credit. Reported examples: **100% financing up to ~$1.5M at a 680 score**, and **up to ~$2M at 720**. Higher scores unlock larger loan amounts, lower down-payment tiers, and better rates.
- **Score → rate:** roughly every **20-point increase above 740** can improve the rate by about **0.125%**. On a $700K loan that is real monthly money over 30 years.
- **Credit mistakes that derail a home purchase in progress:** opening new credit cards / retail cards for a discount, financing a car or furniture, co-signing a loan, or running up card balances — all can move the score or the DTI between pre-approval and closing. **One 30-day late payment in the past two years can drop a score 50–100 points.** Closing old cards shortens credit history and can *hurt* the score.
- **Hard-inquiry timing:** mortgage rate-shopping is protected — multiple mortgage inquiries inside a **14–45 day window** (model-dependent) count as a **single inquiry**. A hard inquiry typically costs only **0–3 points**, and most scoring models only weigh inquiries in the **first 12 months**. Practical move: cluster all hard pulls into a 2–3 week window.

**Today's article topic: the physician homebuyer's credit-score playbook — how physician lenders price credit, the score tiers that gate loan size and rate, the five mistakes that quietly cost approval mid-process, and how to shop without bruising the score.**
