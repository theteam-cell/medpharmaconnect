# Research Notes — 2026-05-24

## 1. Physician Mortgage Rate Outlook for 2026

**Source:** PhysicianLoansUSA, WealthKeel, DrLoanTool

Physician mortgage rates are currently running in the **mid-6% to low-7% range** (30-year fixed), which is approximately 0.125%–0.50% above comparable conventional mortgage rates — a premium sometimes called the "doctor tax." Shopping across 3–5 lenders can reveal rate differences of 0.25%–0.50%, potentially saving thousands annually.

Key dynamics:
- Fannie Mae projects conventional 30-year rates to average ~6.4% by end of 2025, potentially dipping below 6.0% in late 2026.
- Increasing lender competition: more banks and non-bank lenders are offering physician loan programs and expanding eligibility to CRNAs, NPs, and PAs.
- ARMs taken during 2021–2022 (2.5%–3.5% rates) are now adjusting to 5%+, creating payment shock for some physician borrowers.

## 2. Major Federal Student Loan Policy Changes (Effective July 1, 2026)

**Sources:** AAMC, Medical Economics, StudentLoanPlanner

Starting July 1, 2026, federal student aid rules change significantly for new medical borrowers:

- **Borrowing caps**: Direct Unsubsidized Loans capped at $50,000/year, $200,000 total. Grad PLUS program eliminated for new borrowers.
- **Repayment plans**: New borrowers limited to Tiered Standard Plan or Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP). RAP replaces all IDR options going forward.
- **PSLF changes**: Medical residencies no longer count as qualifying public service for new borrowers (those who didn't borrow before June 30, 2025).
- **Mortgage implication**: Borrowers on RAP will have different DTI profiles. Physician loan underwriters may need to adapt to the new repayment plan landscape.

The AAMC has warned these changes could worsen the doctor shortage by increasing the net cost of medical education.

## 3. 2026 Housing Market: A "K-Shaped" Recovery

**Sources:** NewHomeSource, Fortune/Redfin, Newsweek

The 2026 housing market is being described as "K-shaped" — recovering strongly for high-income earners while remaining out of reach for many others.

- Redfin's "Great Housing Reset" forecast: income growth will outpace home-price growth for a prolonged period in 2026 for the first time since the post-2008 era.
- Buyers need to earn ~$111,252/year to afford the typical U.S. home.
- High-income households (including physicians) are the group most likely to reenter the housing market first in 2026.
- Inventory is gradually improving in most metro areas, giving buyers more options than in 2022–2024.

**Takeaway for MedPharmaConnect:** Conditions are relatively favorable for attending physicians entering the market in 2026, but early-career doctors (residents, fellows) still face the same traditional mortgage barriers — high DTI from student loans — making physician loan programs especially relevant.
