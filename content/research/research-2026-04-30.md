# Research Notes — 2026-04-30

## 1. Physician mortgage rates — spring 2026 snapshot
- 30-year fixed physician mortgages tracking in the **mid-6% to low-7%** range depending on LTV, FICO, and loan size.
- Physician loan rates running roughly **0.125%–0.50% above comparable conventional rates** because they're held as portfolio loans (not sold to Fannie/Freddie).
- Lender shopping continues to matter: 3–5 quotes regularly produces **0.25%–0.50% rate dispersion** on identical files — meaningful annual savings on a doctor-sized loan.
- Spring 2026 framed by economists as a genuine opportunity window: easing rates plus rising inventory after years of frozen activity.

## 2. Spring 2026 housing market — inventory loosening, prices anchored
- Months of supply at **4.1 months**, up from 3.8 the prior month — still under "balanced" (5–6 months) but the highest since 2019 in many metros.
- Median existing-home price **$408,800**, up 1.4% YoY — the **33rd consecutive month** of YoY price increases. Translation: inventory is rising faster than prices are correcting.
- Price cuts concentrated in specific metros: **~50% of listings** in North Port-Sarasota, Tampa, and Phoenix have taken at least one price reduction. Boston, Providence, and Detroit are seeing fastest inventory growth.
- First-time buyer activity returning, though affordability still constrained at sub-$400K price points.
- Implication for physicians: the buyer-friendly window is **regional and segment-specific**, not nationwide.

## 3. July 1, 2026 student-loan transition — now ~9 weeks out
- **Grad PLUS phasing out** for new graduate/professional students starting July 1, 2026. Existing borrowers can continue accessing Grad PLUS for up to three more years or until they finish their program.
- New caps for current professional students: **$50,000/year, $200,000 lifetime** in Direct Unsubsidized Loans. Graduate (non-professional) students: $20,500/year, $100,000 lifetime.
- Parent PLUS borrowers working toward PSLF must consolidate before **July 1, 2026** to retain IBR eligibility — Department of Education's recommended application date (April 1, 2026) has already passed; remaining applicants face a tight processing window (30–90 days).
- For physician mortgage applicants, the practical takeaway is the same as the last three weeks: **document your IDR figure and PSLF status in writing now**, while the rules you originally entered the system under are still being applied to your loan.
