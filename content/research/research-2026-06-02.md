# Research Notes — 2026-06-02

## 1. Mortgage rates (June 2026)
- Freddie Mac PMMS 30-yr fixed averaged **6.53%** for the week ending May 28, 2026 (up from 6.51% the prior week) — highest in roughly nine months, ~36 bp below the year-ago 6.89%.
- Daily trackers as of June 1, 2026 show the 30-yr purchase rate around **6.53%–6.58%** (Money daily survey ~6.58%; US News ~6.537%) as the spring buying season peaks.
- Physician-loan band remains **mid-6% to low-7%**, typically carrying a 0.125%–0.50% premium over conventional/jumbo, with meaningful dispersion (0.25%–0.50%) across portfolio lenders on identical files.
- Sources: Freddie Mac PMMS; Money.com daily rates; US News (June 1, 2026).

## 2. Housing market (high-income / spring 2026)
- Inventory in April 2026 ran ~4.6% above a year earlier; broader forecasts call for ~10% inventory growth in 2026 (Realtor.com, Bright MLS). Zillow still notes a ~17% shortfall vs. pre-pandemic norms. Some trackers cite inventory ~20% above a year ago.
- Price-growth forecasts for 2026 are modest and clustered low-single-digit: Zillow +1.2% to +3.5%, Fannie Mae +3.1%, Freddie Mac +2.8%, Realtor.com +2.4%, NAR +2.9%.
- Implication: a calmer, better-supplied market with more negotiating room for buyers — relevant to physicians considering a second/vacation property, where comps can be thinner.
- Sources: JPMorgan 2026 outlook; Zillow Research; NAR; Realtor.com.

## 3. Self-employed / partnership-income physician qualifying
- Physician programs let self-employed doctors qualify on **1 year** of tax returns (vs. the conventional 2 years), often with a YTD profit-and-loss statement.
- Max DTI on physician programs typically ~45% (some to 50%), vs. ~43% conventional — helps doctors carrying student debt and practice-ownership debt.
- Physician loans commonly extend to ~$2M; 0% down, no PMI; partnership (K-1) distributions are counted but treatment varies by lender.
- Sources: WealthKeel; Student Loan Planner; Truss Financial; Fifth Third.
