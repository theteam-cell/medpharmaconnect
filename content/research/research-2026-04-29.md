# Research Notes — 2026-04-29

## 1. Spring 2026 housing market: rates ease, inventory inches up, lock-in effect finally cracking

Late-April 2026 numbers paint a notably more buyer-friendly picture than the last two springs:

- **30-year fixed averaged 6.23% the week of April 23, 2026**, the lowest level of the last three spring buying seasons (down from 6.81% a year ago and 6.64% at the late-March peak this cycle). The 15-year fixed averaged 5.58%.
- **Inventory is loosening.** Unsold inventory hit 1.36M units in March (4.1 months of supply, up from 3.8 in February). Active listings are up 8.1% year-over-year, though still 13.6% below March 2019.
- **Lock-in effect is finally easing.** As of Q3 2025, the share of outstanding mortgages priced at 6%+ exceeded the share below 3% for the first time — meaning fewer would-be sellers feel "trapped" by their old rate. NAR has been flagging this as the structural shift that may finally unlock listings in 2026.
- **Prices remain firm at the top.** Median existing-home price hit a March record of $408,800. NAR has trimmed its 2026 forecast to a 4% rise in existing-home sales (higher rates than originally expected).

Practical implication for the MedPharmaConnect audience: high-income medical professionals — especially those with closing-cost flexibility and stable W-2 attending offers — are entering the most negotiable spring market since 2019, but only at the price points where inventory has actually loosened (suburban single-family $500K–$900K). Luxury and ultra-low-inventory metros remain seller-favored.

## 2. Physician mortgage rates: 6.5%–7.0% band holds, but lender-specific quotes are the real story

Physician-loan pricing in late April 2026 continues to cluster in the **6.5%–7.0%** band for 30-year fixed, with the "doctor tax" (premium over conventional) still running **0.125%–0.50%**. One lender published a 6.125% rate / 6.0% APR quote on April 19, 2026, anchoring the low end of the spread.

Two patterns worth flagging this week:

- **Portfolio pricing means three quotes is the floor.** Physician loans are held on lenders' balance sheets rather than securitized, so the same borrower can see materially different quotes from three lenders on the same day. Industry guidance continues to recommend a minimum of **three** physician-loan quotes before locking, including at least one non-bank.
- **Rate spreads compounding over the life of the loan.** A 0.125%–0.50% premium over a comparable conventional loan adds roughly **$25,000–$30,000 in extra interest on a $500,000 loan over 30 years**, per Bankrate's 2026 guide. That's a useful number for clients deciding whether the physician program's no-PMI / 0%-down feature actually pays for itself in their situation.

Practical implication: the *price* of a physician loan in 2026 is less about the average rate and more about the spread between lenders for an individual file. The biggest lever a borrower controls between today and closing is **shopping** — second only to FICO bracket and DTI presentation.

## 3. July 1, 2026 student loan transition — now ~9 weeks out, RAP details firming

With the July 1, 2026 cutover ~9 weeks away, the picture of what the Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP) actually looks like for medical borrowers is sharpening:

- **RAP becomes the only PSLF-eligible IDR plan for new loans on/after July 1, 2026.** Payments are 1%–10% of AGI (floor of $10/month for AGI under $10K).
- **Forgiveness horizon extends to 30 years** under RAP if a balance remains. RAP includes unpaid-interest relief and a $50/month principal reduction when the calculated payment doesn't cover interest — useful guardrails for residents — but the lack of a payment cap means **attending-stage payments can run materially higher** than under prior IDR plans.
- **Existing borrowers (pre-July-1, 2026 disbursements)** keep IBR access. Borrowers currently on SAVE, PAYE, or ICR have a hard switch deadline of **July 1, 2028** to IBR or RAP, or face auto-enrollment.
- **Parent PLUS borrowers are excluded from RAP** and therefore lose PSLF eligibility on new loans — relevant for medical professionals who co-signed for family members or who themselves have Parent PLUS exposure from their own children's education.

Practical implication for the MedPharmaConnect audience: any resident, fellow, or recently graduated attending who is *also* shopping for a physician mortgage in the next eight weeks should confirm their current IDR plan and PSLF status in writing with their servicer before applying — physician-loan underwriting often relies on the documented IDR figure for DTI, and the conservative move is to lock in that documentation while the rules they're underwritten against are still the ones they entered the system under.
