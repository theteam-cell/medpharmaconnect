# Research Notes — 2026-05-15

Daily scan of news and developments relevant to physician mortgage loans, doctor home buying, student loan policy, and the housing market for high-income medical professionals.

## 1. Spring 2026 mortgage rates remain stubbornly above 6%

The 30-year fixed-rate mortgage averaged **6.37% as of May 7, 2026**, up from 6.30% the prior week, after briefly dipping below 6% at the end of February. Economists now expect rates to bounce between the low- and mid-6% range through the rest of the year. Zillow research attributes a soft spring selling season largely to high financing costs, and geopolitical uncertainty in the Middle East has added to the headwinds. New-home median prices have fallen to their lowest level since July 2021 and inventory is up year-over-year, giving qualified buyers more leverage than they've had in several years.

**Why it matters for our audience:** Physician borrowers shopping this spring are seeing physician-loan fixed rates roughly 0.125%–0.50% above conforming conventional rates, putting most 30-year physician fixed quotes in the **mid-6% to low-7% range**. That premium is the trade-off for 0%–5% down with no PMI and flexible DTI treatment of student debt.

## 2. SAVE plan officially wound down — physician borrowers on a 90-day clock starting July 1

The federal court order that vacated the **Saving on a Valuable Education (SAVE) plan** is now being implemented. Loan servicers will begin issuing **90-day transition notices to roughly 7.5 million SAVE borrowers on July 1, 2026**. Borrowers who don't pick a new plan within 90 days will be auto-enrolled in either the existing Standard Repayment Plan or the new **Tiered Standard Plan**. For *new* federal loan borrowers after July 1, only two plans will exist going forward: Standard Repayment and the new **Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP)**. RAP bases monthly payments on income and dependents and — unlike older IDR plans — guarantees principal reduction for borrowers who pay in full and on time. Professional students (medical, dental, veterinary, law) can borrow up to **$50,000/year and $200,000 total** under new caps.

**Why it matters for our audience:** Physician mortgage underwriters lean heavily on the *documented* monthly student loan payment when calculating DTI. A change in repayment plan can swing a borrower's qualifying payment by hundreds of dollars per month. Residents and recent attendings currently in SAVE need to map out their July–October decision before they sign a purchase contract.

## 3. Physician mortgage product landscape continues to broaden

Multiple lender roundups published in early 2026 confirm physician mortgage programs are still expanding, not contracting, even in a higher-rate environment. Common 2026 features highlighted across "best of" guides: **0% down up to ~$1M, no PMI up to ~$2M, expanded eligibility for residents and fellows, and DTI treatment that lets underwriters use IDR payments — or in some cases exclude student debt entirely** during deferment. A growing number of programs also extend physician loan terms to dentists (DDS/DMD), pharmacists (PharmD), and in some cases nurse anesthetists and PAs, though terms vary widely.

**Why it matters for our audience:** The product is competitive again. Borrowers who shopped one bank in 2023 and assumed all physician loans were similar should re-shop in 2026 — loan limits, rate spreads, and condo eligibility differ meaningfully across lenders.
