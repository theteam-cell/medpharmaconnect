# Research Notes — 2026-05-19

## 1. Rates — Freddie Mac PMMS and physician-loan band

- Freddie Mac PMMS 30-yr fixed averaged **6.36%** the week of May 14, 2026 — down 1 bp w/w from 6.37% and ~45 bp below spring 2025 (6.81%). 15-yr fixed averaged **5.71%**, also down 1 bp w/w.
- Daily indices remained range-bound 6.30%–6.51% through the second week of May; consensus May–July clustered **5.9%–6.5%**, with conventional 30-yr potentially dipping just below 6.0% by late 2026 (Fannie Mae forecast).
- Physician-loan band continues to run **mid-6% to low-7%**, with a **0.125%–0.50% "doctor tax"** premium over conventional and **0.25%–0.50% lender dispersion** on identical files. The practical refi threshold for spring-2024/2025 physician-loan originations is still not expected before late-2026/early-2027.
- Tier breaks reaffirmed in 2026 best-of guides: a 680→760+ FICO move is worth 0.25%–0.50% on physician-loan pricing, and loans above ~$1M typically carry a 0.125%–0.25% bump versus the $300K–$750K core band.

## 2. Rate buydown economics — sharper definition in 2026 buyer-leaning market

- **2-1 buydown** (rate –2% in year 1, –1% in year 2, note rate years 3+): costs **2%–3% of the loan amount**. Example: ~$7,600–$11,400 on a $380K loan, ~$10K–$15K on a $500K loan.
- **3-2-1 buydown** (rate –3%, –2%, –1% across years 1–3): costs **3%–4% of the loan amount**. Example: ~$14K–$18K on a $400K loan; ~$20K–$25K on a $700K loan — fits within most concession limits.
- **Permanent discount points**: 1 point = 1% of loan, typically buys ~0.25% off the rate; break-even windows usually 4–6 years at current physician-loan pricing.
- **Seller concession ceilings**: 3% of purchase price at >90% LTV, up to 6% at ≤75% LTV under most physician-loan portfolio programs (lender-specific variation). In 2026, **~68% of sellers reported offering concessions** and **~35% of 2026 buyers received them**, averaging ~$8,500 per deal — leaving room to negotiate a buydown structure instead of (or alongside) a price cut.
- **Unused buydown escrow is refundable**: if the borrower refinances or sells before the temporary buydown period ends, remaining funds are typically credited to payoff — meaning a seller-funded 2-1 doesn't strand cash if rates fall enough to refi in year 2.

## 3. Student-loan cutover — RAP transition now ~6 weeks out

- SAVE wind-down is in motion: ~7.5M borrowers will receive 90-day transition notices starting July 1 with auto-enrollment into Standard or new Tiered Standard. RAP becomes the only IDR option for **new borrowers after July 1, 2026**; pre-July borrowers retain IBR as an alternative.
- RAP payments are **1%–10% of AGI uncapped**, $10 minimum, with 30-yr forgiveness window. Example: a $500K-AGI attending on RAP runs ~$4,167/mo vs. ~$2,000/mo on capped IBR — a swing material enough to push DTI past portfolio limits on physician-loan files closing after July 1.
- Physician-loan banks continue to **pre-underwrite projected RAP payments** for any file closing after July 1, with several requiring written income-driven payment scenarios from the loan officer before issuing a final pre-approval. This is now the dominant DTI variable on otherwise-clean files.
