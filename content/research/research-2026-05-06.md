## Item 2 — Refinance economics in 2026: the break-even rules of thumb

- Refi closing costs in 2026 generally run **2%–5% of loan amount** ($10,000–$25,000 on a $500K physician loan). On a $750K jumbo physician loan, that's $15K–$37K of closing-cost drag to recover.
- The standard rule: divide total refi costs by monthly P&I savings → break-even in months. If costs are $12,000 and savings are $400/mo, break-even is 30 months (2.5 years).
- "**No-cost refis**" aren't free — the lender takes back the closing costs through a 0.25%–0.50% higher rate. Worth it only if you expect to move or refi again within 3–5 years. For attendings who plan to stay 7+ years, the higher-rate version is usually the worse trade.
- Discount points: 1 point (1% of loan) typically buys ~0.25% of rate improvement. On $500K, that's $5,000 upfront for ~$84/mo savings — a 5-year break-even, similar to closing-cost recovery on a no-point refi.
- Common physician-loan-specific wrinkles: some portfolio lenders **won't refi their own loans** (they want you to leave the relationship to refi), and some allow refis only into conventional/jumbo products, which means you may lose the 0% / 5% / 10% down structure but gain a meaningfully lower rate.

Why it matters: the 0.75%–1.0% rate-improvement rule of thumb assumes ~30 months break-even on standard closing costs. Physicians who closed at high rates with **lender credits** already baked in (i.e., paid almost nothing at close) face a *different* break-even — they don't have a sunk closing-cost handicap to amortize, so even a 0.50% rate drop may be net-positive.

## Item 3 — Student-loan policy clock: July 1, 2026 is now ~8 weeks out

- The RAP (Repayment Assistance Plan) cutover on **July 1, 2026** is now ~8 weeks away. Key terms: 1%–10% of AGI, **no payment cap**, 30-year horizon. For a $500K-AGI attending, that's **$417/mo to $4,167/mo** depending on tier — a much wider band than IBR/PAYE.
- For PSLF: residency and fellowship payments **may no longer count** toward the 120 under post-July rules for new borrowers. Borrowers whose loans existed before July 1, 2026 at a qualifying employer should still get residency credit under existing rules.
- Grad PLUS goes away July 1; new federal limits for med/dental/pharmacy students drop to **$50K/yr / $200K lifetime**.
- For **refinance underwriting**: lenders are increasingly pre-underwriting **dual DTIs** — current IDR payment vs. projected RAP payment — to stress-test approval. A refi that pencils today on the IDR DTI may not pencil after July 1 on the RAP DTI. If you're considering a refi, **closing before July 1** can lock in a cleaner DTI picture for the file.

Why it matters: timing matters for both sides of the refi math — rate side and DTI side. Attendings on PSLF with high balances should run the RAP-DTI scenario before deciding whether to refi a physician loan in the next 8 weeks.
