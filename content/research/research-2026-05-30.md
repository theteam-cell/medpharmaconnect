## 1. Mortgage rate environment (late May 2026)
- Freddie Mac PMMS 30-year fixed averaged **6.53%** for the week ending May 28, 2026, up from 6.51% the prior week (week ending May 21). A year ago the 30-year FRM averaged 6.89% — still a modest ~36 bp year-over-year decline.

## 2. TOPIC OF THE DAY — Visa-holder / IMG physicians and the mortgage market (FRESH, uncovered angle)
- International medical graduates (IMGs) make up roughly a quarter of practicing US physicians; many work on **H-1B**, **J-1** (often with a Conrad 30 / waiver pathway), or **O-1** visas before reaching a green card.
- **2025 HUD rule change:** non-permanent residents (including most H-1B holders) are **no longer eligible for FHA-insured loans**. This removes a low-down-payment fallback and makes the **physician (portfolio) mortgage and conventional loans the primary route** for visa-holder doctors — a timely reason to highlight the product.
- **H-1B:** generally the most lender-friendly visa thanks to "dual intent." Eligible for conventional and for many physician/doctor-loan programs. Typically need a credit score around 620+, ~2 years steady work history (residency counts at many physician lenders), and a reasonable amount of time left on the visa (rule of thumb: ≥1 year).
- **J-1:** harder. Exchange-visitor status and the two-year home-residency requirement make some lenders cautious; J-1 borrowers often face **higher down payments (20–25%)** or need a physician lender comfortable with the visa class. Conrad 30 waiver status helps.
- **O-1 / TN / green-card-pending:** case by case; the stronger the path to permanence and the longer the remaining authorization, the better.
- Specialized physician lenders (e.g., physician-loan desks at regional banks, and some non-bank physician programs) explicitly market to visa-holder doctors and can offer **0% or low down with no PMI** where a generic conventional lender would demand more — this is the core value proposition for the article.
- Sources: MedicalProfessionalHomeLoans.com (visa-holder page); JVM Lending (mortgages for visa holders / immigrant homebuyer guide); Manifest Law H-1B mortgage guide (2026); Reliance Financial (H-1B + FHA); Scotsman Guide (loan options after HUD eligibility change); White Coat Investor doctor-loan guide.
