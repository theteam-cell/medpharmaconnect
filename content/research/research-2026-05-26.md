# Research Notes — May 26, 2026

## 1. Physician Mortgage Rate Outlook for 2026

**Source:** PhysicianLoansUSA, WealthKeel, PhysicianLiving

Physician mortgage fixed rates in 2026 are running approximately **0.125%–0.50% above comparable conventional rates**, with a 30-year fixed physician mortgage typically falling in the **mid-6% to low-7% range** depending on loan-to-value, credit score, and loan amount. Economists project modest rate declines toward the low-6% range by late 2026, improving affordability after years of rate-driven stagnation. More lenders are now competing in the physician loan space — including non-bank lenders — and some programs are expanding eligibility to CRNAs, nurse practitioners, and physician assistants. Core features (0% down, no PMI) remain intact across most programs.

**Angle for content:** Great time to compare physician loans vs. conventional while rates are still elevated but trending down; physician loan's PMI savings are especially compelling right now.

## 2. Major Federal Student Loan Changes Effective July 1, 2026

**Source:** Medical Economics, StudentLoanPlanner, HumbleWealth, AAMC

A wave of significant federal student loan policy changes takes effect July 1, 2026 under the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" framework:

- **New RAP repayment plan launches** (Repayment Assistance Plan) — will count toward PSLF and replaces SAVE, PAYE, and ICR (which sunset in July 2028)
- **IBR closed to new borrowers** — any federal loans taken after June 30, 2026 are ineligible for IBR; existing borrowers are grandfathered
- **Grad PLUS eliminated** — no longer available for new borrowers after July 1, 2026
- **Unsubsidized loan cap** — $50,000/year and $200,000 lifetime for graduate borrowers
- **Residency/fellowship PSLF credit removed** — time in training will no longer count toward the 120-payment PSLF requirement

**Angle for content:** These changes are highly relevant to medical residents considering a home purchase — their student loan repayment trajectory is shifting. The physician mortgage's flexibility around student debt DTI calculations becomes even more valuable in this environment.

## 3. Housing Market Conditions for Medical Professionals in 2026

**Source:** PhysicianLoansUSA 2026 Housing Market Outlook

Top economists describe 2026 as a window of opportunity: lower mortgage rates and rising housing inventory are beginning to loosen a market that has been stagnant. Affordability won't bounce back quickly, but the direction is favorable. For high-income medical professionals buying in the $600K–$1.2M range, jumbo physician loan programs with no PMI are especially attractive given the gap between conventional conforming limits and typical physician home prices.

**Angle for content:** Physicians who delayed buying during 2023–2025 rate peaks may find 2026 a better entry point; also worth noting that physician loan programs handle jumbo amounts well where conventional lenders impose stricter terms.
