# Research Notes — 2026-05-03 (Sunday)

## Item 1 — Physician mortgage rate & pricing environment heading into May 2026

- 30-yr fixed physician mortgage band remains **mid-6% to low-7%** in early May 2026 — most quotes landing **6.25%–7.00%** depending on LTV, FICO, loan size, and whether the lender is a portfolio bank, credit union, or non-bank.
- Premium over comparable conventional fixed: roughly **+0.125% to +0.50%** on standard physician files, widening to **+0.25% to +0.75%** on jumbo / high-LTV / lower-FICO files.
- **Lender dispersion of 0.25%–0.50% on identical files** continues to be the single biggest source of cost variance — the case for shopping 3–5 lenders is unchanged. Industry estimates put the lifetime savings on a $50K+ over a 30-year term for the high-vs-low quote on the same file.
- Structural backdrop: **Basel III phase-ins** through 2026 increase capital required against high-LTV portfolio loans, which is part of why physician-loan spreads over conventional are widening rather than compressing — even as the broader rate curve eases. Banks are becoming more selective on physician originations or pricing the capital cost into the rate.
- Practical refi threshold for most current physician-mortgage borrowers is still **~6.0%** with normal closing costs — and consensus across lender desks is that we are unlikely to see that level sustained before late-2026 / early-2027. Translation: most 2025–2026 physician-mortgage borrowers should plan to live with their current note for at least another 12–18 months.

## Item 2 — Spring 2026 housing market: thaw is real, but uneven

- Freddie Mac PMMS for the week ending April 23, 2026: **30-yr fixed at 6.23%** — the lowest level during any spring homebuying season in three consecutive years.
- April 30 reading bumped to **6.30%** on geopolitical / oil-driven volatility (rates briefly dipped under 6% earlier this spring before climbing back above 6.5% mid-March, then easing again).
- **YoY:** 30-yr fixed is roughly **46 bp below** the same week in spring 2025 (6.76%).
- **Inventory:** ~1.36M units / **4.1 months of supply** in March (up from 3.8 in February) — still below the ~6 months considered balanced. NAR estimates 300K–500K additional homes needed for true balance.
- **Lock-in effect cracking:** Coldwell Banker's spring 2026 survey of 700+ agents finds **35% of current sellers hold a rate below 5%** and are listing anyway — life circumstances are beginning to override the lock-in math. **43% of agents** report a busier season than 2025.
- **Regional split persists:** Midwest/Northeast holding seller-friendly conditions; South/West (Tampa, Phoenix, Sarasota, parts of Texas) trending buyer-friendly with elevated price cuts. NAR's 2026 outlook calls for modest **2%–4% national price growth** with mortgage rates trending lower across the year.

## Item 3 — PSLF / RAP transition: 8.5 weeks to July 1, 2026

- **The Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP)** becomes the only IDR available to **new borrowers** taking loans after **July 1, 2026.** RAP counts as a qualifying plan for PSLF.
- **Existing borrowers** (anyone who completes medical/dental/pharmacy school before July 2026) retain access to **IBR** alongside RAP — a meaningful choice for high-income trainees because IBR has a payment cap and RAP does not.
- **Big change for trainees:** Reporting and lender commentary continues to flag that **residency/fellowship payments may no longer count toward PSLF** under the new framework — historically a major value driver for the doctor-PSLF math. This remains the single most important policy item for residents and fellows to confirm with their loan servicer and a student-loan specialist before July 1.
- **Employer eligibility:** Starting July 1, 2026, the Department of Education has new authority to disqualify employers from PSLF eligibility under a "substantial illegal purpose" standard — a category that could, in theory, sweep in some nonprofits and teaching institutions that previously qualified. Doctors targeting PSLF should plan for periodic re-verification of employer status.
- **Mortgage-side implication:** Physician-mortgage underwriters are increasingly running **dual DTI calculations** — one using the borrower's current IBR/PAYE/SAVE-equivalent payment, one using a projected RAP payment (which has no income cap and can run several thousand dollars/month for $400K+ AGI households). Borrowers closing between now and ~July 1 should ask up front which payment the lender will use, and request a pre-approval that survives a RAP-based recalc.
