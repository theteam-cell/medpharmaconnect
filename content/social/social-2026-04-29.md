## LinkedIn

Most physician-mortgage articles answer "*can* I qualify?"

The harder, more useful question is "*should* I buy now, or wait 12 months?"

For doctors, dentists, and pharmacists, four levers usually decide it:

1. Training stage — and whether your income is about to step-change.
2. Contract certainty — a signed offer is what underwrites a future paycheck.
3. Geographic stability — honest 5–7-year confidence in the city.
4. Policy windows — the July 1, 2026 student-loan transition is now ~9 weeks out.

A good purchase has at least three pointing the same way. A great one has all four.

The single highest-leverage window for most physicians isn't residency or fellowship — it's the **12 months around the signed attending offer**. Most physician-loan programs let you close 60–90 days before the start date on contract income, your DTI is at its cleanest, and you haven't taken on lifestyle debt yet.

Spring 2026 specifically is sharpening this picture: 30-year fixed averaged 6.23% last week (lowest of the last three spring seasons), inventory is up 8.1% YoY, and the lock-in effect is finally easing.

If you're in that signed-contract window in a price band with real inventory — pay attention. Full breakdown on MedPharmaConnect.
