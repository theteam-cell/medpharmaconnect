## LinkedIn (professional, insightful)

Most physician-mortgage content is written for a $400K–$700K purchase.

But if you're a dual-physician household buying in Boston, the Bay Area,
San Diego, Seattle, or coastal South Florida, your realistic price point is
$1.4M, $2.1M, or $2.8M — and above the 2026 conforming line ($832,750
baseline, $1,249,125 in high-cost areas), the physician mortgage stops
behaving like a single product and becomes a tiered ladder.

A representative 2026 ladder, simplified:

— Up to ~$1M: 0% down, no PMI, standard program
— $1M–$2M: 5%–10% down, 3–6 months PITI in reserves, modest rate add-on
— $2M–$3M: 10%–15% down, 6–12 months PITI in reserves, FICO 720+ common
— $3M–$3.5M: 20% down, FICO 740+, 12+ months reserves

The physician product still wins in this range for most buyers — no PMI,
IDR/RAP-friendly student loan treatment, contract-based qualification — but
the margin is narrower than at $450K, and the cost of being on the wrong
rung of the ladder is much higher.

Three asks for every lender you quote:

1. Which rung am I on, and what's the down payment, FICO floor, and reserve
requirement for that rung — in writing?
2. How are my student loans qualified (current IDR, projected RAP, or
fully-amortized), and what changes if I close after July 1, 2026?
3. Where does your conventional or non-bank jumbo beat your physician
program on all-in 5- and 10-year cost?

## Facebook (friendly, accessible)

If you're a doctor or dentist looking at a home over $1M, the "physician
mortgage" you've read about online probably isn't the version you'll
actually qualify for.

Most articles describe the $500K, 0% down, no-PMI product. Once your loan
crosses the 2026 conforming line ($832,750 baseline, $1.25M in high-cost
metros), the physician program turns into a ladder — different down
payments, FICO floors, and cash reserve requirements at each rung.

The good news: at $1M, $1.5M, and $2M, the physician product is still one
of the cleanest jumbo paths on the market, especially if you have heavy
student loans. The catch is that the rules change as the loan size grows,
and the new RAP student loan plan (live July 1, 2026) can swing your
qualifying purchase price by $200K+ on a big file.

Our newest article walks through the ladder at $1M, $2M, and $3M — and the
six questions to ask every lender before you write an offer.
