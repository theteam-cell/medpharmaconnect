## LinkedIn (professional, insightful)

Spring 2026 has produced the widest ARM-vs.-fixed discount on physician mortgages we've seen this cycle: 0.50% to 1.00% off the 30-year fixed for 5/6 and 7/6 ARMs. On a $500K loan, that's $200–$400/month during the fixed period.

Before you take the discount, four questions decide whether it's the right call:

1. How confident are you that you'll move or refinance before the fixed period ends?
2. Can your budget absorb the worst-case reset under the cap structure (often 2/1/5 on a 5/6 ARM — a 2-point jump at first reset, 5-point lifetime cap)?
3. Are you stockpiling the monthly savings — or letting them drift into lifestyle?
4. What's the breakeven if you stay past the reset?

For PGY-2s buying near their training program with a known relocation, the 5/6 ARM often does exactly what it was built to do. For attendings putting down long-term roots, the fixed-rate premium is usually worth it for the payment certainty.

The ARM discount is real money. The cap structure is real risk. The right product depends on which one fits your actual life — not the marketing.
