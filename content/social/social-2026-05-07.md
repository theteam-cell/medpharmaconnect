## LinkedIn

July 1, 2026 is a bigger date for physician home buyers than most realize.

Three federal changes are landing at once:

- Graduate PLUS loans are being eliminated for new borrowers
- Professional students will be capped at $50K/year and $200K lifetime in federal loans
- PSLF is tightening, with new authority to disqualify certain employers

Why does this matter if you are already through school?

Because the entire reason physician mortgage loans exist is that lenders treat student debt differently than conventional underwriting does. As IDR rules and federal loan structures shift, so will the way physician lenders interpret them.

If you are even six to twelve months from buying, three things are worth doing this month:

1. Pull a fresh pre-approval under current rules
2. Get your lender’s IDR treatment in writing
3. If you are pursuing PSLF, confirm your employer’s status today

This is not a reason to rush a purchase. It is a reason to have an informed conversation while the rules are still the rules you planned around.

(Educational only — not financial advice.)
