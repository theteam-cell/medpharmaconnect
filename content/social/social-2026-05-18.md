## LinkedIn

The marketing on most physician-mortgage products leads with MDs and DOs. Pharmacists are listed as an afterthought — when they're listed at all. The reality in 2026 is more useful: many physician-mortgage banks do extend their programs to PharmDs, and the no-PMI, low-down-payment structure can be just as valuable to a pharmacist with $167,000 of school debt as it is to a third-year resident.

But the product behaves differently for pharmacists than it does for MDs, and treating them as identical is how files end up either at the wrong tier or denied after the credit pull. Three things to know going in:

1. Pharmacist eligibility is bank-by-bank, not industry-wide. Confirm in the underwriting guideline, not on the marketing page.
2. Most pharmacists qualify on W-2 income — not the future-contract pathway MDs use. If you're pre-paycheck, you need the smaller subset of banks that handle pharmacist contract closes.
3. RAP launches July 1. On a $150K pharmacist AGI, projected RAP runs $750–$1,000/month — enough to move a DTI from comfortably approved to needs-a-co-borrower. Lenders are already pre-underwriting RAP for post-July-1 closings.
