## LinkedIn

Most physicians don't lose money on their mortgage because their credit is bad. They lose it because their credit is in the wrong tier.

Physician-mortgage rate sheets in 2026 step at predictable FICO thresholds — typically 680, 700, 720, and 740. Industry rule of thumb: about 0.125% rate improvement for every ~20 FICO points above 740, capped around 760–780 depending on the lender. With non-bank lenders pushing into the physician-loan market this spring, the spread on identical files between lenders is now running as wide as 0.50–0.625%, and a meaningful share of that gap is FICO-driven.

If you're at 718, getting to 720 is real money. If you're at 738, getting to 740 is real money. Three high-leverage moves in the 3–6 months before you apply:

1. Pay revolving balances down *before* the statement closing date — utilization is reported on close, not on due date. Target under 10% in aggregate.
2. Don't open or close anything for six months. No new cards, no auto loans, no balance transfers.
3. Get a written statement from your student-loan servicer documenting your current IDR payment. With the July 1, 2026 federal loan transition coming, the documented figure will anchor your DTI well into 2027.

You don't need an 800 FICO. You need a clean file that lands in the right bracket — and the discipline to leave it alone for six months while it gets there.
