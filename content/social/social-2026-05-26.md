## LinkedIn

After three years of rate peaks and thin inventory, the 2026 housing market is showing real signs of improvement for physician buyers — and a few factors specific to medical professionals make this moment worth paying attention to.

Here's what's shifted:

**Rates are down ~45 basis points** from spring 2025. Still not the 3% era, but meaningfully better math on a $700K–$1.2M purchase.

**Inventory is at its highest since 2019.** More options, less competition, more negotiating room.

**Income growth for physicians has finally outpaced home-price appreciation** — projected at 0–3% nationally this year — for the first time in several years.

But there's also a policy clock running. On July 1, federal student loan rules change significantly, including how income-driven repayment payments will be calculated. For physicians with large student balances, this could shift how lenders calculate your debt-to-income ratio — affecting your qualifying purchase price.

A 2026 purchase window for physicians isn't a reason to rush. Geographic stability, career certainty, and the right financial picture still matter most. But for those who've been sitting on the sidelines since 2023 waiting for conditions to improve: this is a meaningfully different environment.
