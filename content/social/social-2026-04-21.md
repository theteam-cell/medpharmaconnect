## LinkedIn

Almost every physician mortgage explainer is written for physicians. Dentists get a sentence confirming eligibility, and then the article moves on — assuming a five-year residency, a match letter, and a salaried academic attending role.

The product does include DDSs and DMDs. But the shape of a dental career is different enough that several parts of the standard playbook need to change:

- Your underwriting document is usually an associate or DSO contract, not a match letter.
- Collections-based compensation is handled very differently across lenders — ask each one how they'll calculate your qualifying income before anything else.
- If you're planning to acquire a practice, the practice loan sits on your personal credit file and affects home-loan DTI. The order of operations — home first, practice second, or vice versa — genuinely matters.
- Dental graduates now carry around $310K in student debt on average, higher than MDs. Physician-loan programs still use actual IDR payment, but dentists are less likely to have a PSLF path, which changes the math.

As of spring 2026, several programs have expanded dentist-specific loan limits — up to $2.5M at low down payments for established dentists. Worth shopping 3–5 programs specifically.
