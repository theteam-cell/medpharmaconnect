## LinkedIn

Rates haven't moved much. Freddie Mac's 30-yr fixed averaged 6.36% the week of May 14 — physician-loan band still mid-6% to low-7%. So the conversation has shifted from "is the rate going to drop" to "who is paying to lower it."

A few clarifying points for medical professionals weighing buydowns on a physician mortgage:

A 2-1 buydown costs 2%–3% of the loan amount and lowers your rate by 2% in year 1 and 1% in year 2 before reverting. A 3-2-1 stretches that to three years for 3%–4% of the loan. Permanent discount points cost roughly 1% of the loan per ~0.25% of permanent rate reduction — break-even usually 4–5 years.

Three details that change the answer:

1. Most physician-loan portfolio programs cap seller concessions at 3% of purchase price above 90% LTV — exactly where most attending borrowers sit. A 3-2-1 on a $700K loan can blow through that limit fast.

2. Lenders qualify your DTI on the note rate, not the buydown rate. With the RAP cutover on July 1 already pulling projected student-loan payments into underwriting, a buydown that fixes your monthly payment may not save a DTI-tight file.

3. Unused buydown escrow is typically refundable to the payoff if you sell or refinance early — so the math is less binary than it looks.

The right buydown structure is a match between your hold period, your concession ceiling, and your post-July student-loan picture. Not a product.
