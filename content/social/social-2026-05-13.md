## LinkedIn (professional, insightful)

Building a home as a physician is a different conversation than buying one — and a lot of doctors learn that the hard way.

A physician construction loan combines the no-PMI, low-down, contract-friendly underwriting of a physician mortgage with the staged, draw-based funding of a construction loan. But there are two flavors, and the choice matters in 2026.

**One-time close (OTC):** You close once, before the foundation is poured. Permanent-loan rate is locked from day one for 12+ months. Slightly higher rate, but one set of closing costs and zero rate risk during the build.

**Two-time close:** Separate construction loan, then a permanent refi at completion. Two closings. Rate isn't locked until the home is finished — you reprice into whatever the market does.

With the 30-year fixed at 6.37% this week (up from 6.30%), and forecasts clustered at 5.9%–6.5% by late summer, OTC has a slight edge for most builders right now. The exception: a long build (14+ months) where you genuinely expect rates to fall.

Four physician-specific wrinkles to plan around:

1. The construction-loan lender list is shorter than the purchase-loan list. Plan on 2–3 quotes, not 5.
2. Pharmacists are included by fewer construction programs than purchase programs — confirm before you fall in love with a plot.
3. The new federal Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP) goes live July 1, 2026. Because your construction loan won't close for 12+ months, lenders are increasingly pre-underwriting projected RAP payments — which can swing qualifying purchase price by $50K–$150K on a high-debt, high-AGI file.
4. The builder's preferred lender often steers you off the physician program and back to conventional with PMI. Quote both paths side by side.

Pick the structure first, then the lender.

## Facebook (friendly, accessible)

Building a home as a doctor, dentist, or pharmacist? Your physician mortgage may have a sibling you've never met: the physician construction loan.

Same vibe — no PMI, low-to-zero down, contract income counts — but the structure is different. You can either close once (locking your rate before the foundation is poured) or twice (locking your rate when the home is done). One isn't universally better. With rates at 6.37% this week and forecasts hovering 5.9%–6.5% into late summer, the "close once" route is winning more often right now.

Three quick things to know:
🏗️ Fewer banks offer this product than offer the purchase version — plan on 2–3 quotes.
📚 The new student loan rules (RAP) start July 1, 2026, and your build will close after that — get the lender's DTI math under RAP, not your current payment.
🤝 The builder's preferred lender isn't always your best lender — quote both side by side.

Pick the structure first. Pick the lender second. Then break ground.
