## LinkedIn
You shopped three lenders, locked a great physician-loan rate, and ran the principal-and-interest number. Then the closing disclosure arrives — and the payment is $600 higher than you quoted your spouse.

Nothing went wrong. You just met escrow.

Property taxes and homeowners insurance don't care about your interest rate. The same $700K home can carry $200/month in property tax in one state and $1,000+/month in another. Insurance premiums in higher-risk regions can run 4–5x a low-risk policy. Together, escrow routinely adds $400–$1,500 to a monthly payment the rate alone never hinted at.

The fix isn't complicated: before you make an offer, get the actual tax figure for the specific property and a real insurance quote — not regional averages. Divide by twelve, add to your P&I, and budget off that PITI number.

A physician loan can spare you PMI and a down payment. It can't shrink your county's tax rate. Run the full number before you sign, not after.
