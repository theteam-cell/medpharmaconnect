## LinkedIn (professional, insightful)

Most "physician mortgage" articles are written for MDs coming out of residency with a W-2 contract in hand.

That is not the average dental borrower in 2026.

If you are a DDS or DMD, the product usually works for you — but three things bend the math in ways your lender may not volunteer:

1) **1099 income.** Associate-dentist 1099 pay and practice ownership trigger the two-year self-employment rule. Physician programs waive that rule for W-2 offer letters. Most do not waive it for 1099.

2) **Student loans without PSLF.** The class of 2025 graduated with roughly $297,800 of dental-school debt. The new federal Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP) takes effect July 1, 2026 — uncapped 1%–10% of AGI — and most private-practice dentists do not qualify for PSLF as a backstop. Lenders are increasingly pre-underwriting projected RAP payments rather than your current IDR amount, which can cut qualifying purchase price by $50K–$100K.

3) **The practice loan nobody asks about until underwriting.** Some banks exclude practice-loan payments if they are serviced from operating cash flow. Others count the full payment against you. Ask in writing before you give a hard pull.

The spring 2026 backdrop is real — 30-year fixed at 6.37%, inventory up about 8% YoY, wage growth finally outpacing home-price growth. But the leverage in your file comes from shopping three to five lenders and making each of them answer those three questions about your situation specifically.

## Facebook (friendly, accessible)

If you are a dentist thinking about buying a home in 2026, here is something almost no "physician loan" article will tell you.

The product was designed around a hospital-employed MD with a W-2 contract. Most banks do not adjust it for the way dental careers actually look — 1099 associateships, practice buy-ins, and student loans without PSLF as a safety net.

The fix is not complicated. Shop more lenders than your friends did, ask each of them in writing how they will count your 1099 income, your projected RAP student-loan payment after July 1, and your practice loan if you have one. Then compare.
