## LinkedIn (professional, insightful)

Physician mortgage coverage obsesses over student debt. It treats your credit score as the easy part.

It usually is — until it quietly isn't.

A physician mortgage isn't pass/fail on credit. It's tiered, and each tier buys you something different:

→ The program floor runs ~660–720 across lenders. Clearing it gets you in the door — not the best pricing.
→ The generous structures key off your score: reported 2026 examples show 100% financing up to ~$1.5M at a 680, and up to ~$2M at 720.
→ Rate ladder: roughly every 20 points above 740 improves your rate by about 0.125%. On a $700K loan, that compounds for decades.

A 712 and a 762 both "qualify." They are not buying the same mortgage.

And the real danger isn't your starting score — it's the 30–60 days between pre-approval and closing, when lenders re-pull your credit. Five mistakes do most of the damage:

1. Financing a car or furniture for the new house
2. Opening a retail card for the checkout discount
3. A single 30-day late payment (can cost 50–100 points)
4. Closing old cards (shortens history, raises utilization)
5. Co-signing a loan

With the 30-year fixed flat at 6.36%, the rate environment is out of your hands. Your credit file isn't. Freeze it from pre-approval to closing — no new accounts, no closed accounts, no missed payments.

The physician who manages their own credit file beats the one waiting for the market to move.

## Facebook (friendly, accessible)

Buying a home this year? Here's a tip that saves doctors real money — and it has nothing to do with your student loans.

Your credit score on a physician mortgage isn't a simple yes or no. It works in tiers. Clearing the minimum (usually around 700) gets you in the program. But pushing higher unlocks bigger loan amounts, smaller down payments, and a better rate — roughly 0.125% better for every 20 points above 740.

And here's the part people miss: the most dangerous time for your credit is AFTER you're pre-approved. Lenders re-check your credit right before closing. So between pre-approval and the keys, don't:

🚫 Finance a car or furniture for the new place
🚫 Open a store card for the checkout discount
🚫 Miss a payment (one late can cost 50–100 points)
🚫 Close old credit cards
🚫 Co-sign anyone's loan

Freeze your credit profile and keep it boring until you've closed. Your future self will thank you. 🏡
