## LinkedIn

Major federal student loan changes take effect July 1, 2026 — and if you're a physician, dentist, or pharmacist thinking about buying a home, the timing matters.

Here's what's changing:

→ Grad PLUS loans are being eliminated for new borrowers
→ Federal loan limits drop to $200,000 total — well below typical medical school debt
→ Residency/fellowship years will no longer count toward PSLF
→ Income-driven repayment is being consolidated into a single new plan (RAP)

Current borrowers are largely grandfathered, but these changes will ripple through how future physicians manage debt — and how lenders evaluate them.

For those already attending: now is a good time to make sure you understand how your student loans are being counted in your mortgage application. Physician mortgage programs were built for exactly this kind of financial complexity.
