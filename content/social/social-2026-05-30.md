## LinkedIn (professional, insightful)

International medical graduates make up about a quarter of practicing US physicians — and many spend years on an H-1B, J-1, or O-1 visa before they ever reach a green card.

A quiet 2025 rule change reshaped their path to homeownership: HUD removed FHA eligibility for most non-permanent residents. The low-down-payment fallback many visa-holder doctors counted on is largely gone.

The upside? It pushes international physicians toward the product that often serves them best anyway — the physician mortgage, with 0%-to-low down and no PMI, offered by lenders who already understand offer-letter income and non-permanent residency.

Three things that consistently matter for visa-holder physicians:
• H-1B is the most lender-friendly visa, thanks to "dual intent"
• J-1 is workable but often means a larger down payment — a Conrad 30 waiver strengthens the file
• Who you apply with matters more here than almost anywhere in lending — quoted terms vary widely

Our latest piece breaks down how each visa class is treated and what to have ready before you apply. Educational only — we're not a lender. Link in comments.
