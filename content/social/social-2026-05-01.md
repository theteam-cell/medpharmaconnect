## LinkedIn (professional, insightful)

**Same FICO. Same offer letter. Same down payment. $50,000 difference.**

That's the spread we're seeing in spring 2026 between the cheapest and most expensive physician-mortgage quotes on identical files.

Three forces are pulling quotes apart:

1. Non-bank entrants like Newrez are now in the broker channel, competing head-on with bank portfolio programs (BMO, Fifth Third, TD, First Horizon, Truist).
2. Lenders are pricing tighter to FICO bands — a 738 and a 742 can land in different rate sheets at the same lender.
3. Student-loan DTI treatment varies wildly. Same borrower, three lenders, three different DTIs.

A reliable shopping playbook for doctors, dentists, and pharmacists:

→ Build the file once (W-2s, pay stubs / offer letter, two months of statements, a one-page student-loan summary).
→ Cast a five-lender net: 3 bank portfolio programs + 1 broker + 1 credit union or regional lender.
→ Cluster all credit pulls within a 14-day window so FICO treats them as one inquiry.
→ Compare four numbers on each Loan Estimate: note rate, APR, lender fees in Section A, total cash to close.
→ Walk the leader's LE back to your #2 and ask for a match.

A 0.25% rate improvement on a $600K loan over 30 years is roughly $30,000. For a process that takes about a week of an organized buyer's time, that's a high hourly wage.

Educational — not a quote, not advice, not a lender.

## Facebook (friendly, accessible)

If you're a doctor, dentist, or pharmacist house-hunting this spring — please don't stop at the first physician-mortgage quote you get.

Right now, two physicians with the same credit score and the same job offer can get rate quotes that are **0.25%–0.50% apart** from different lenders. On a $600,000 loan over 30 years, that gap is **$25,000 to $50,000** out of your pocket.

A simple weekend project that pays for itself:

1. Get 3 bank doctor-loan quotes + 1 broker quote + 1 credit union quote.
2. Make all the calls in the same 2 weeks (so your credit score doesn't take multiple hits).
3. Ask each one for a "Loan Estimate" — it's a standard form that lets you compare apples to apples.
4. Take the cheapest one back to your second-favorite and say, "Can you match this?"

You'd be amazed how often the answer is yes.

This isn't a lender — just a resource for medical professionals trying to make smart decisions in a confusing market.
