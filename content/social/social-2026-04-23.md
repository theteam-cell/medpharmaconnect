## LinkedIn

Pharmacists are eligible for most "physician mortgage" programs — 0% down, no PMI, portfolio-priced — but the product was designed around MD career timing and debt profiles. Most explainers mention pharmacists in passing and then move on.

A few things that matter specifically for PharmDs:

- The term "physician mortgage" is a marketing label, not an eligibility category. First Merchants, Flagstar, Fulton Bank, BMO, Armed Forces Bank, Physician Bank, and Regions' "Emerging Professionals" program all include pharmacists — but the loan limits, down payment floors, and LTV caps aren't always identical to the MD version of the same product.
- Average PharmD debt (~$170K–$180K) is lower than the MD average, and starting salaries ($120K–$140K, higher in hospital/industry) make DTI cleaner from day one. For most pharmacists, the product's real value is the 0%-down / no-PMI structure, not the student-loan math.
- A signed employment offer with a licensure contingency is usually underwritable. A verbal offer is not. Ask the question before the offer letter is drafted.

New this spring: non-bank lenders (Redwood, Newrez) are entering the physician mortgage market at scale. Shopping 3–5 programs across both bank and non-bank origination is now the highest-leverage move.
