## LinkedIn

One of the most misunderstood facts in medical personal finance: a signed employment contract can qualify as income on a physician mortgage — often 60 to 120 days before your first attending paycheck clears.

That single underwriting rule is why residents and fellows can realistically close on a home before their start date, without waiting two years for W-2 history the way almost every other borrower has to.

A few things worth knowing if you're in that window right now:

- Your contract generally needs to be fully executed, with salary and start date on the page.
- Most physician-loan programs will use your actual IDR student loan payment — not the 1% of balance figure conventional lenders often impute.
- Physician mortgages are portfolio-priced. Two lenders will quote the same borrower rates that differ by 0.25–0.50%. Shopping 3–5 lenders is the highest-leverage move you can make.
- The approval ceiling and the right purchase price are rarely the same number. First-year attending cash flow looks very different from the spreadsheet.

Used thoughtfully, the physician mortgage is one of the few products in finance genuinely designed around how doctors actually earn.
