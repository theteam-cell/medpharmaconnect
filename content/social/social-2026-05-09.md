## LinkedIn

The headlines on physician mortgages get all the attention: 0% down, no PMI, student loans treated favorably.

The underwriting lever that quietly derails more closings is none of those. It is **cash reserves** — the months of PITI you can show in eligible accounts after closing.

Three things worth knowing:

1. The physician baseline is unusually generous (0–6 months, sometimes waived up to ~$2M loan amount), but it is not unlimited.

2. Reserves get flexed up in five predictable situations: lower FICO, future-income contract close, jumbo balances, 1099/self-employed income, and variable-income concentration. A low-700s FICO can move the ask to 6–12 months. Closing pre-paycheck typically adds 3–6 months on top.

3. What you have on paper is not what counts. Brokerage typically gets a 30% haircut, vested retirement a 40% haircut (and sometimes is not counted at all), crypto often counts for zero, business accounts are excluded without documentation. Run the numbers with the haircuts before assuming you are covered.

Practical target for most physician borrowers in 2026: **3–6 months of PITI(A) post-closing**, with residents and fellows closing on contract income aiming for the upper end.

The physician mortgage product was designed to forgive a thin balance sheet on day one of attending life. Forgiving is not unlimited.

The one number that quietly torpedoes more physician mortgage closings than rates or appraisals is **cash reserves** — how much you have left in the bank AFTER your down payment and closing costs.

Most physician programs are flexible about it (sometimes 0 months required). But the second something else looks risky — lower credit score, closing on a contract before your first paycheck, jumbo loan amount, 1099 income — lenders quietly bump the ask to 6 or even 12 months of mortgage payments in the bank.

Worse: not all your money "counts." Brokerage accounts get a 30% haircut, retirement balances often get a 40% haircut (or zero), and crypto usually counts for nothing.

→ Checking & savings: 100%
