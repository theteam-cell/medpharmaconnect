## LinkedIn
The share of physicians working locum tenens has nearly tripled in a decade — from about 5% to 14%. It's no longer a gap-filler; it's a deliberate career choice.

But there's a catch that surprises a lot of contract doctors: the moment your income shows up on a 1099 instead of a W-2, conventional mortgage underwriting gets nervous. Lenders often want two years of tax returns and then average your *net* income — after all the deductions your CPA worked hard to capture. A physician billing $400K can look, on paper, like they earn far less.

Physician mortgage programs were built for this. Several lenders will:
• Qualify locum doctors on a signed contract, sometimes without two years of returns
• Count your actual income-driven student loan payment (not an inflated percentage of the balance)
• Keep the 0% down, no-PMI structure that lets you stay liquid between assignments

If a home purchase is on your horizon, the single most useful move is talking to your CPA *before* you apply — aggressive deductions help in April and hurt in underwriting.
