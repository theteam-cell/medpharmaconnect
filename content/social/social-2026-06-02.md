## LinkedIn (professional, insightful)
You used a physician mortgage for your first home and loved it — 0% down, no PMI, an underwriter who didn't flinch at your student loans. So when the lake house or a rental down the street comes up, the instinct is to do it again.

Here's what most doctors get wrong: nearly every physician mortgage program in 2026 is restricted to a **primary, owner-occupied residence**. The favorable terms exist precisely because the home you live in is the lender's lowest-risk loan. A second home or rental breaks that logic — so the physician-loan playbook generally doesn't apply.

What actually works for a second property: a conventional second-home loan (~10–20% down), an investment-property loan (15–25% down, ~0.5–0.875% higher rate), home equity from your primary residence, or — the one real exception — a second physician mortgage when you're genuinely relocating and will live in the new home.

One caution worth repeating: certifying owner-occupancy on a home you won't live in is occupancy fraud, and lenders can call the whole loan due. Plan the financing before you make the offer.
