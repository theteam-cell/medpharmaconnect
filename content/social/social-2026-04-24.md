## LinkedIn

A quiet truth in physician home-buying: the physician mortgage is often the right answer — but not always.

We see three scenarios where doctors, dentists, and pharmacists are better served by a conventional loan:

→ You're putting 20% or more down (no PMI either way; conventional usually comes in 0.125%–0.50% cheaper on rate).
→ You have limited or no student debt (the DTI advantage disappears).
→ You plan to refinance or sell within 3–5 years.

And one scenario where the physician mortgage is almost never the right call: you're a military physician eligible for a VA loan. The VA product — 0% down, no mortgage insurance, typically the lowest rates on the market — quietly beats the physician mortgage for nearly every eligible borrower.

The right question isn't "what loan fits my degree?" It's "what loan fits my down payment, my debt, and my timeline?"

Ask your lender to quote both. The numbers will tell you the answer.

## Facebook

Quick reality check for doctors, dentists, and pharmacists shopping for a mortgage: the "physician loan" isn't automatically the best product for you.

It's the right answer when you have big student loans on an income-driven plan, limited liquid savings, or want to avoid 20% down and PMI. That covers a lot of residents and early-career attendings.

But if you're putting 20% or more down, have modest or paid-off student debt, or you're a military veteran eligible for a VA loan — a different product is usually cheaper over the life of the loan.

Our rule of thumb: get quotes on both conventional and physician from at least three lenders before you decide. On portfolio products like physician mortgages, rate spreads between lenders are wider than on conventional — and you'll only see that if you shop.
