## LinkedIn

**Tone:** Professional, insightful

If you're a physician planning to buy a home — or still weighing your options — July 1, 2026 is a date worth circling on your calendar.

Federal student loan rules are changing significantly in just a few weeks:

→ Medical residencies may no longer count toward PSLF for new borrowers
→ Grad PLUS loans are being eliminated
→ Federal unsubsidized loans capped at $200K lifetime
→ Repayment options consolidate to two plans

For many physicians, this reshapes the financial planning picture around homeownership. But here's the good news: physician mortgage programs were designed for exactly this kind of complexity.

Unlike conventional underwriting, physician loans often exclude deferred student loans from DTI calculations, use income-driven payment figures, and allow zero down — no PMI required. A loan balance that disqualifies you under conventional guidelines often looks very different through a physician mortgage lens.

Now is a good time to revisit your loan landscape, talk to a physician-focused financial advisor, and understand what you'd qualify for as a buyer.
