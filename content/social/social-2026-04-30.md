## LinkedIn

Roughly four in ten married physicians are married to another physician or healthcare professional. Yet most physician-loan content is still written for a single applicant.

The default — "we're married, so we both go on the loan" — is one of the most common unforced errors in two-physician households. Three structures actually exist:

— Both on the loan, when both incomes are stable and needed to qualify.
— One on the loan, one on title, when one spouse is mid-transition (residency-to-attending, job change, contract gap) and you want to protect the rate.
— One on the loan, one off title entirely, in narrow estate or community-property situations.

The decision is financial, not symbolic. The home is jointly yours either way. The question is just how the lender prices the loan — because they'll price to the lower of the two FICOs and the household's combined DTI.

Spring 2026 makes this worth getting right. With months of supply at 4.1 (highest since 2019 in many metros), physician-loan rates in the mid-6% range, and lender dispersion still showing 0.25%–0.50% spreads on identical files, structure decisions made before the application can swing tens of thousands in lifetime interest.
