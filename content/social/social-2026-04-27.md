## LinkedIn

The physician mortgage is built to be forgiving. 0% down, no PMI, and a DTI calculation that respects how medical professionals actually carry student debt.

But the same features that make it forgiving also make it easy to misuse.

The seven most expensive mistakes we see — across residents, attendings, dental associates, and clinical pharmacists:

1. Treating the first quote as the right quote (lender spreads on physician programs are running 0.625% on identical files in spring 2026).
2. Borrowing the full pre-approval amount instead of building a real monthly cash-flow plan first.
3. Closing before the executed employment contract — or its effective date — is in writing.
4. Letting IDR student-loan documentation lapse before underwriting, especially heading into the July 1, 2026 federal student-loan transition.
5. Confusing "no PMI" with "no escrow surprises."
6. Skipping the side-by-side conventional quote when 20%+ down is on the table.
7. Forgetting that spring 2026 is the most buyer-friendly market we've seen since 2019 — and not asking for closing-cost credits or rate buy-downs.

The physician mortgage rewards borrowers who treat it like the powerful tool it is. Shop it, document everything, and run the numbers on the alternatives before you sign.

→ Closing without a fully signed employment contract
→ Skipping the conventional quote when you have 20%+ down
→ Forgetting that in spring 2026, buyers can ask for closing-cost credits and rate buy-downs again
