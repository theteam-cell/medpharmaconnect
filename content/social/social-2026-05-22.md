**Tone: Professional, insightful**

New federal student loan rules (effective July 1, 2026) are reshaping the financial landscape for physicians, dentists, and pharmacists — and if you're planning to buy a home in the next few years, they're worth understanding.

The short version: Grad PLUS loans are being eliminated for new borrowers, annual federal borrowing limits are dropping to $50,000, and PSLF residency credit is going away for new borrowers. More doctors will carry a mix of federal and private loans with less flexible repayment terms.

Here's the silver lining most people miss: physician mortgage loans account for this. Their DTI underwriting excludes or minimizes student debt in ways conventional loans don't. As the student loan picture grows more complex, that advantage compounds.

If you're a resident, fellow, or early attending navigating these changes, understanding how physician mortgages handle your debt profile could meaningfully change what you can afford — and when.
