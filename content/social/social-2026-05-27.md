## LinkedIn

The physician mortgage market just got a lot more interesting — and a lot more competitive.

Redwood Trust recently closed a **$482 million securitization of medical-professional mortgages**, and lenders like Newrez have launched physician loan products through the wholesale broker channel. For the first time, the physician mortgage isn't just a bank-portfolio product. Non-bank lenders are in the game.

That's good news for borrowers — more competition usually means more leverage on rate and terms. But it also means "physician mortgage" is a label that now gets applied to products with meaningfully different underwriting rules.

Before you let any lender pull your credit, get clear answers — in writing — on two things:

➊ Is this loan held in portfolio, or will it be sold after closing?  
➋ How exactly will my student loan payment be treated in DTI, especially with the July 1 RAP transition?

The rate spread between the best and worst quote on an identical physician mortgage file is currently 0.25%–0.50%. On a $700K loan, that's real money. Shop 3–5 lenders, compare Loan Estimates apples-to-apples, and don't mistake brand recognition for the best economics.

## Facebook

Did you know the physician mortgage market changed significantly this year? 🏠

Big mortgage companies are now competing alongside traditional banks for physician loans — and while that's mostly great news for doctors, dentists, and pharmacists buying homes, it also means "physician mortgage" doesn't mean the same thing from every lender.

Here's what to watch for:
- Can you close using just your employment offer letter? (Some newer entrants still require paystubs.)
- How do they treat your student loans in the debt-to-income calculation?
- Will they hold your loan or sell the servicing after closing?

The good news: more competition = more leverage. Rate spreads between lenders on identical files are wider than they've been in years. Getting 3–5 quotes inside a 14-day window can make a real difference.
