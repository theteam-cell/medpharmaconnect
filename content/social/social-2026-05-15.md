## LinkedIn

July 1, 2026 is a quiet but consequential date for any physician carrying federal student loans.

That's when servicers begin issuing 90-day notices to ~7.5 million SAVE plan borrowers. After that window, you'll either pick a new repayment plan — Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP), Tiered Standard, or 10-year Standard — or one will be picked for you.

The mortgage angle most doctors miss: physician loan underwriters use your *documented* monthly student loan payment when calculating DTI. A SAVE payment of ~$420 auto-rolling into a $2,650 Standard payment can quietly shrink a $400K home approval into a "let's revisit the loan amount" conversation.

If you're closing on a home this year, three steps are worth taking now:

1. Pull side-by-side payment estimates from studentaid.gov in June.
2. Share them with your physician-mortgage loan officer *before* writing an offer.
3. Decide whether to switch plans before or after your loan application — the sequence matters.

The product hasn't gotten worse. The paperwork just got more time-sensitive.
